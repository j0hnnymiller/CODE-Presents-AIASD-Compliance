## Principal Gaps

1. Required legacy risk management activities are summarized but not operationalized as a checklist aligned to 4.4.2.
2. Gap analysis does not define the comparison baseline against the deliverables expected from 5.2, 5.3, 5.7, and clause 7.
3. The minimum deliverable and risk-based decision logic for closure priorities are not explicit.
4. Objective evidence criteria for continued use and for reconstructed deliverables are not defined.
5. Linkage to problem resolution and maintenance processes is referenced but not sufficiently proceduralized.

## Assessment Narrative

The SOP has the right structure for a legacy software pathway, but it does not yet provide the level of operational detail that auditors will expect when reviewing how a project determines what historical evidence is sufficient, what must be recreated, and how remaining uncertainty is accepted. The largest weakness is not recognition of the standard, but execution detail. A project team could follow section 6.1 and still produce materially inconsistent legacy evidence packages across products.

## Recommended Remediations

1. Add a clause 4.4 appendix that enumerates the five mandatory legacy risk management activities and the required evidence for each.
2. Add a deliverable matrix by safety class showing the minimum expected legacy documentation set and the decision rules for additional closure.
3. Require a documented gap analysis report with explicit comparison to 5.2, 5.3, 5.7, and clause 7 outputs.
4. Define acceptable objective evidence sources for reconstructed records, including tests, issue histories, design reviews, and field data.
5. Add explicit handoffs to sections 6.10 and 6.13 when gap closure identifies open defects or needed changes.

- Immediate: add the legacy evidence checklist and gap analysis matrix.
- Near term: define objective evidence and approval criteria for continued use.
