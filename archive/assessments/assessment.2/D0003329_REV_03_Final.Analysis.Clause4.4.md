## Executive Summary

D0003329 Rev 03 Section 6.1 provides foundational coverage of IEC 62304 Clause 4.4 (Legacy Software) requirements but exhibits significant gaps in procedural detail, risk management integration, and practical implementation guidance. The work instruction correctly identifies the alternative compliance pathway (§4.4.2-4.4.5 vs. §5-9) and addresses key requirements at a high level. However, critical deficiencies exist in risk management activities specification, gap analysis methodology, gap closure planning, and documentation of rationale.

**Critical Finding**: The work instruction lacks sufficient procedural detail to ensure consistent, auditable execution of legacy software compliance activities, particularly in risk management assessment and gap analysis methodology.

## Section 4.4: Legacy Software

### 4.4.1: General - Software Safety Classification Determination

**IEC 62304 Requirement (§4.4.1):**

> "As an alternative to applying Clauses 5 through 9 of this standard, compliance of LEGACY SOFTWARE may be demonstrated as indicated in 4.4.2 to 4.4.5."

**Compliant Elements** ✅

1. **Alternative Compliance Pathway Identified**

   - **D0003329 Reference**: §6.1, paragraph 1
   - **Evidence**: "For medical device software that was developed for which there is insufficient objective evidence that it was developed according to the current BS EN 62304 standard, compliance may be demonstrated by either: Applying BS EN 62304 Clauses 4.4.2 through 4.4.5, or Applying BS EN 62304 Clauses 5 through 9"
   - **Assessment**: Correctly identifies the two compliance pathways for legacy software

2. **Recognition of Less Burdensome Approach**
   - **D0003329 Reference**: §6.1, paragraph 2
   - **Evidence**: "Applying BS EN 62304 Clauses 4.4.2 through 4.4.5 may be a less burdensome approach"
   - **Assessment**: Acknowledges the practical benefit of the legacy software pathway

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

1. **Software Safety Classification Requirement**

   - **IEC 62304 Context**: §4.4.3 requires gap analysis "based on the software safety class of the LEGACY SOFTWARE (see 4.3)"
   - **D0003329 Reference**: General reference in preceding text to Software Safety Classification
   - **Gap**: Section 6.1 does not explicitly state that legacy software must be classified according to §4.3 (A, B, or C) as prerequisite to applying §4.4 requirements
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Recommendation**: Add explicit statement: "Prior to applying §4.4, the legacy software system shall be classified according to BS EN 62304 §4.3 (see Appendix 10.1 Software Safety Classification). The classification determines the scope of required deliverables in the gap analysis (§4.4.3)."

2. **Decision Criteria for Pathway Selection**
   - **Gap**: No guidance provided on when to use §4.4 vs. full §5-9 compliance
   - **D0003329 Reference**: Missing
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Impact**: Without clear decision criteria, teams may inconsistently apply the legacy software pathway, leading to audit findings or inadequate compliance
   - **Recommendation**: Add decision criteria such as:
     - Software was on market before [specific date when IEC 62304 compliance became required]
     - Insufficient objective evidence exists of original IEC 62304 compliance
     - Cost-benefit analysis supports §4.4 pathway
     - Risk assessment confirms §4.4 approach provides equivalent safety assurance

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

None for §4.4.1 general requirement.

### 4.4.2: Risk Management Activities for Continued Use

**IEC 62304 Requirement (§4.4.2):**

> "In accordance with 4.2 of this standard, the MANUFACTURER shall:
> a) assess any feedback, including post-production information, on LEGACY SOFTWARE regarding incidents and / or near incidents, both from inside its own organization and / or from users;
> b) perform RISK MANAGEMENT ACTIVITIES associated with continued use of the LEGACY SOFTWARE, considering the following aspects:
>
> - integration of the LEGACY SOFTWARE in the overall MEDICAL DEVICE architecture;
> - continuing validity of RISK CONTROL measures, implemented as part of the LEGACY SOFTWARE;
> - identification of HAZARDOUS SITUATIONS associated with the continued use of the LEGACY SOFTWARE;
> - identification of potential causes of the LEGACY SOFTWARE contributing to a HAZARDOUS SITUATION;
> - definition of RISK CONTROL measures for each potential cause of the LEGACY SOFTWARE contributing to a HAZARDOUS SITUATION."

**Compliant Elements** ✅

1. **High-Level Risk Management Requirement Stated**
   - **D0003329 Reference**: §6.1, bullet 1 under "Applying BS EN 62304 Clauses 4.4.2 through 4.4.5"
   - **Evidence**: "Perform risk management activities associated with the continued use of legacy software, incorporating any available feedback including post-production information including incidents and near incidents."
   - **Assessment**: Addresses the requirement at a general level, mentioning both risk management and feedback assessment

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

1. **Insufficient Detail on Feedback Assessment (§4.4.2.a)**

   - **IEC 62304 Specific Requirement**: "assess any feedback, including post-production information, on LEGACY SOFTWARE regarding incidents and / or near incidents, both from inside its own organization and / or from users"
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: Mentions "incorporating any available feedback including post-production information including incidents and near incidents"
   - **Gap Description**:
     - No specification of sources to check (complaint databases, field reports, CAPA records, MDR/MAUDE, customer feedback)
     - No timeframe specified for historical review
     - No process for documenting the assessment
     - No linkage to existing post-market surveillance processes
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Recommendation**: Add procedural detail:
     - "Sources to assess: complaint database, CAPA system, field service reports, customer feedback, regulatory databases (MDR/MAUDE), vigilance reports"
     - "Review feedback from software initial release to present"
     - "Document assessment findings in risk management file per D0003329 §6.11"
     - "Cross-reference to D0003098 §6.8 Post Release activities and post-market surveillance"

2. **Missing Specific Risk Management Activities (§4.4.2.b)**
   - **IEC 62304 Specific Requirements**: Five specific aspects must be considered:
     1. Integration in overall medical device architecture
     2. Continuing validity of risk control measures
     3. Identification of hazardous situations
     4. Identification of potential causes
     5. Definition of new risk control measures
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: Generic statement only - "Perform risk management activities"
   - **Gap Description**: None of the five specific aspects are enumerated or explained
   - **Risk Level**: Critical
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Recommendation**: Expand §6.1 to explicitly list and describe each aspect:
     - "Assess how legacy software integrates with current device architecture, including interfaces, data flows, and dependencies"
     - "Verify that previously implemented risk control measures remain valid and effective in current use environment"
     - "Identify hazardous situations that could arise from continued use, considering changes in regulatory environment, standards, or clinical practice since original development"
     - "For each hazardous situation, identify potential software causes or contributors"
     - "Define and implement new risk control measures as needed based on current risk assessment"

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

1. **No Reference to Risk Management Process (§4.2)**

   - **IEC 62304 Context**: §4.4.2 begins "In accordance with 4.2 of this standard..." requiring integration with overall risk management process
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: §6.1 does not reference D0003329 §6.11 (Software-Specific Elements of Risk Management Process) or establish linkage to overall device risk management
   - **Gap Description**: Disconnected requirement without integration into established risk management framework
   - **Risk Level**: Critical
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Recommendation**: Add explicit cross-reference:
     - "Legacy software risk management activities shall be performed in accordance with BS EN 62304 §4.2 and documented per D0003329 §6.11 (Software-Specific Elements of Risk Management Process)"
     - "Risk management activities shall be integrated into the overall device risk management file per ISO 14971 and D0003098 risk management requirements"

2. **No Process Owner or Responsibility Assignment**

   - **Gap**: Section 6.1 does not specify who is responsible for performing legacy software risk assessment
   - **D0003329 Reference**: Missing
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Impact**: Ambiguous accountability could lead to incomplete execution
   - **Recommendation**: Clarify that R&D (per §3 Responsibilities) leads legacy software risk assessment with Quality and SME participation

3. **No Documentation Requirements Specified**
   - **Gap**: No specification of where/how risk management activities are documented
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Recommendation**: Add: "Document risk assessment in Risk Management File with clear identification of legacy software-specific hazards, causes, and control measures. Use risk traceability matrix to link legacy software risks to requirements and verification."

### 4.4.3: Gap Analysis of Available Deliverables

**IEC 62304 Requirement (§4.4.3):**

> "Based on the software safety class of the LEGACY SOFTWARE (see 4.3), the MANUFACTURER shall perform a gap analysis of available DELIVERABLES against those required according to 5.2, 5.3, 5.7, and Clause 7.
> a) The MANUFACTURER shall assess the continuing validity of available DELIVERABLES.
> b) Where gaps are identified, the MANUFACTURER shall EVALUATE the potential reduction in RISK resulting from the generation of the missing DELIVERABLES and associated ACTIVITIES.
> c) Based on this evaluation, the MANUFACTURER shall determine the DELIVERABLES to be created and associated ACTIVITIES to be performed. The minimum DELIVERABLE shall be SOFTWARE SYSTEM test records (see 5.7.5)."

**Compliant Elements** ✅

1. **Gap Analysis Requirement Stated**

   - **D0003329 Reference**: §6.1, bullet 2
   - **Evidence**: "Perform a gap analysis based on the Software Safety Classification to determine the required deliverables missing."
   - **Assessment**: Acknowledges need for gap analysis and links to safety classification

2. **Implicit Recognition of Minimum Testing Requirement**
   - **D0003329 Context**: While not explicitly stated in §6.1, overall document structure (§6.8 Software System Testing) provides framework for test records
   - **Assessment**: Partial - infrastructure exists but not explicitly called out as minimum requirement for legacy software

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

1. **No Specification of Required Deliverables by Safety Class**

   - **IEC 62304 Requirement**: Gap analysis against deliverables required per §5.2 (Requirements), §5.3 (Architecture), §5.7 (System Testing), and Clause 7 (Risk Management)
   - **Expected Deliverables by Class**:
     - **Class A**: Software requirements (§5.2), system test records (§5.7.5)
     - **Class B**: Above plus software architecture (§5.3), risk control measures in requirements (§5.2.3)
     - **Class C**: Above plus detailed design (§5.4), additional risk management activities (Clause 7)
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: Generic statement "to determine the required deliverables missing" without enumeration
   - **Gap Description**: No checklist, matrix, or reference table showing specific deliverables required by safety class
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C) - increases with class
   - **Recommendation**: Add table or appendix:

   | Safety Class | Required Deliverables for Gap Analysis                      | IEC 62304 Reference    |
   | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
   | Class A      | Software requirements, System test records                  | §5.2, §5.7.5           |
   | Class B      | Above + Architecture, Risk control measures in requirements | §5.3, §5.2.3, Clause 7 |
   | Class C      | Above + Detailed design, Risk analysis for software items   | §5.4, §7.1, §7.2       |

2. **No Methodology for Assessing Continuing Validity (§4.4.3.a)**

   - **IEC 62304 Specific Requirement**: "assess the continuing validity of available DELIVERABLES"
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: Not addressed
   - **Gap Description**: No guidance on how to determine if existing legacy documentation remains valid, accurate, and applicable
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Assessment Criteria Missing**:
     - Are requirements still current and complete?
     - Does architecture accurately represent current implementation?
     - Are test records still relevant given current regulatory environment?
     - Have device changes invalidated legacy deliverables?
   - **Recommendation**: Add assessment criteria:
     - "For each available deliverable, assess: (1) Accuracy - does it reflect actual software? (2) Completeness - does it meet current standard requirements? (3) Currency - is it still applicable given device evolution? (4) Traceability - can it be linked to current verification?"
     - "Document validity assessment in gap analysis report"

3. **No Risk-Benefit Evaluation Process (§4.4.3.b)**

   - **IEC 62304 Specific Requirement**: "Where gaps are identified, the MANUFACTURER shall EVALUATE the potential reduction in RISK resulting from the generation of the missing DELIVERABLES and associated ACTIVITIES"
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: Not addressed
   - **Gap Description**: No process for evaluating cost/benefit of generating missing deliverables versus accepting residual risk
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: Primarily B and C (Class A has minimal deliverables)
   - **Impact**: Without risk-benefit evaluation, organizations may either:
     - Over-invest in generating unnecessary deliverables, or
     - Under-invest and miss critical safety documentation
   - **Recommendation**: Add evaluation framework:
     - "For each identified gap, evaluate: (1) Safety impact - what risk reduction would the deliverable provide? (2) Feasibility - can the deliverable be reliably reconstructed? (3) Resource requirement - effort to generate? (4) Regulatory expectation - is this deliverable typically requested in audits/submissions?"
     - "Document evaluation in gap closure plan with justification for generate vs. accept gap decision"

4. **Minimum Deliverable Not Explicitly Required**
   - **IEC 62304 Specific Requirement**: "The minimum DELIVERABLE shall be SOFTWARE SYSTEM test records (see 5.7.5)"
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: Not stated in §6.1
   - **Gap Description**: No explicit statement that system test records are mandatory minimum regardless of other gaps
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Recommendation**: Add to §6.1: "At minimum, SOFTWARE SYSTEM test records (per BS EN 62304 §5.7.5) shall be generated regardless of other gap closure decisions. If original test records cannot be validated, system testing shall be performed on current legacy software version."

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

1. **No Gap Analysis Template or Tool Reference**

   - **Gap**: No template, checklist, or tool specified for performing gap analysis
   - **D0003329 Reference**: Missing
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Impact**: Inconsistent gap analysis execution across projects; difficulty demonstrating systematic approach in audits
   - **Recommendation**:
     - Create template/form: "D000XXXX, Global Template, Legacy Software Gap Analysis"
     - Reference in §6.1: "Gap analysis shall be documented using [template reference] or equivalent, capturing: (1) Required deliverables per safety class, (2) Available deliverables, (3) Validity assessment, (4) Identified gaps, (5) Risk-benefit evaluation, (6) Gap closure decisions"

2. **No Integration with D0003098 Design History File Requirements**

   - **Gap**: No linkage between legacy software gap analysis and DHF requirements in D0003098
   - **D0003329 Reference**: Missing
   - **D0003098 Context**: §6.1 Design History File requirements
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Impact**: Legacy software documentation may not be properly integrated into DHF, creating traceability gaps for regulatory submissions
   - **Recommendation**: Add: "Gap analysis results and generated deliverables shall be incorporated into the Design History File (DHF) per D0003098 §6.1. Legacy software documentation shall be clearly identified and version-controlled within DHF structure."

3. **No Timeframe or Triggers for Gap Analysis**
   - **Gap**: No specification of when gap analysis must be performed
   - **Risk Level**: Minor
   - **Recommendation**: Add triggers:
     - "Gap analysis shall be performed when: (1) Legacy software is incorporated into new device design, (2) Existing device with legacy software undergoes major modification, (3) Regulatory requirements change requiring re-assessment, (4) Post-market data indicates safety concerns"

### 4.4.4: Gap Closure Activities and Plan Execution

**IEC 62304 Requirement (§4.4.4):**

> "a) The MANUFACTURER shall establish and execute a plan to generate the identified DELIVERABLES. Where available, objective evidence may be used to generate required DELIVERABLES without performing ACTIVITIES required by 5.2, 5.3, 5.7 and Clause 7.
> NOTE: A plan on how to address the identified gaps can be included in a software maintenance plan (see 6.1).
> b) The plan shall address the use of the problem resolution PROCESS for handling problems detected in the LEGACY SOFTWARE and DELIVERABLES in accordance with Clause 9.
> c) Changes to the LEGACY SOFTWARE shall be performed in accordance with Clause 6."

**Compliant Elements** ✅

1. **Plan Establishment Requirement Stated**
   - **D0003329 Reference**: §6.1, bullet 3
   - **Evidence**: "Establish and execute a plan to generate the identified deliverables."
   - **Assessment**: Acknowledges need for plan and execution

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

1. **No Plan Content Requirements Specified**

   - **IEC 62304 Context**: §4.4.4.a requires plan to "generate the identified DELIVERABLES"
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: States requirement but provides no guidance on plan content
   - **Gap Description**: No specification of what the gap closure plan must contain
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Missing Elements**:
     - List of deliverables to be generated
     - Methods for generating each deliverable
     - Resources and timeline
     - Acceptance criteria for generated deliverables
     - Review and approval process
   - **Recommendation**: Add plan content requirements:
     - "Gap closure plan shall include: (1) List of deliverables to generate with rationale, (2) Method for generating each (new creation vs. reconstruction from objective evidence), (3) Timeline and milestones, (4) Resource allocation, (5) Acceptance criteria, (6) Review and approval workflow"
     - "Plan may be standalone or incorporated into Software Maintenance Plan (per D0003329 §6.10)"

2. **Use of Objective Evidence Not Addressed**

   - **IEC 62304 Specific Provision**: "Where available, objective evidence may be used to generate required DELIVERABLES without performing ACTIVITIES required by 5.2, 5.3, 5.7 and Clause 7"
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: Not mentioned
   - **Gap Description**: Important flexibility provision not explained, potentially leading to unnecessary rework
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: Primarily B and C (higher documentation burden)
   - **Impact**: Teams may unnecessarily perform full lifecycle activities instead of using existing evidence
   - **Examples of Objective Evidence**:
     - Legacy test results, bug reports, change history
     - Source code with embedded comments
     - User manuals, training materials
     - Field performance data, complaint analysis
     - Design review minutes, email discussions
   - **Recommendation**: Add provision:
     - "Where possible, use objective evidence to generate deliverables without repeating lifecycle activities. Examples of acceptable objective evidence include: source code analysis, legacy test results, change control records, design review documentation, field performance data."
     - "Document the source and validity of objective evidence used to generate each deliverable."

3. **Integration with Software Maintenance Plan Not Mentioned**
   - **IEC 62304 Note**: "A plan on how to address the identified gaps can be included in a software maintenance plan (see 6.1)"
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: §6.1 (Legacy Software) does not reference §6.10 (Software Maintenance)
   - **Gap Description**: No guidance on whether to create standalone plan or integrate with maintenance plan
   - **Risk Level**: Minor
   - **Recommendation**: Add option: "Gap closure plan may be standalone document or integrated into Software Maintenance Plan (per D0003329 §6.10). For legacy software continuing in production, integration with maintenance plan is recommended."

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

1. **No Problem Resolution Process Integration (§4.4.4.b)**

   - **IEC 62304 Specific Requirement**: "The plan shall address the use of the problem resolution PROCESS for handling problems detected in the LEGACY SOFTWARE and DELIVERABLES in accordance with Clause 9"
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: Not addressed in §6.1
   - **Gap Description**: Critical requirement completely missing - no linkage to problem resolution process
   - **Risk Level**: Critical
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **D0003329 Context**: §6.13 describes Software Problem Resolution Process per BS EN 62304 Clause 9
   - **Impact**: Problems discovered during gap closure activities may not be properly tracked and resolved
   - **Recommendation**: Add mandatory element:
     - "Gap closure plan shall specify use of Problem Resolution Process (per D0003329 §6.13) for handling: (1) Problems discovered in legacy software during gap analysis or testing, (2) Discrepancies found in legacy deliverables, (3) Issues identified during deliverable generation"
     - "All problems shall be documented, analyzed, and resolved per BS EN 62304 Clause 9 before gap closure is considered complete"

2. **No Linkage to Configuration Management for Changes (§4.4.4.c)**

   - **IEC 62304 Specific Requirement**: "Changes to the LEGACY SOFTWARE shall be performed in accordance with Clause 6"
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: Not addressed in §6.1
   - **Gap Description**: No statement that any changes to legacy software during gap closure must follow software change control
   - **Risk Level**: Critical
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **D0003329 Context**: §6.12 describes Software Configuration Management per BS EN 62304 Clause 8
   - **Impact**: Changes to legacy software could be made outside formal change control, invalidating compliance and introducing defects
   - **Recommendation**: Add mandatory requirement:
     - "Any changes to legacy software identified during gap closure activities shall be performed per Software Configuration Management process (D0003329 §6.12) and BS EN 62304 Clause 6 (Maintenance Process)"
     - "Changes include: bug fixes discovered during testing, risk control measure implementation, architectural or design corrections"
     - "Each change shall trigger impact assessment to determine if gap analysis must be repeated or expanded"

3. **No Plan Approval or Review Requirements**

   - **Gap**: No specification of who reviews/approves gap closure plan
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Recommendation**: Add approval requirement referencing D0003329 §3 Responsibilities: "Gap closure plan shall be reviewed by Quality and SME, and approved by R&D prior to execution."

4. **No Verification of Generated Deliverables**
   - **Gap**: No requirement to verify that generated deliverables are adequate and accurate
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Recommendation**: Add verification step: "Generated deliverables shall be verified for: (1) Completeness against IEC 62304 requirements, (2) Accuracy relative to actual legacy software, (3) Consistency with other deliverables, (4) Traceability to requirements and risks. Verification results shall be documented before deliverables are finalized."

### 4.4.5: Documentation of Rationale for Continued Use

**IEC 62304 Requirement (§4.4.5):**

> "The MANUFACTURER shall document the VERSION of the LEGACY SOFTWARE together with a rationale for the continued use of the LEGACY SOFTWARE based on the outputs of 4.4.
> NOTE: Fulfilling 4.4 enables further use of LEGACY SOFTWARE in accordance with IEC 62304."

**Compliant Elements** ✅

1. **Version Documentation Requirement Stated**
   - **D0003329 Reference**: §6.1, bullet 4
   - **Evidence**: "Document the version of the legacy software with a rationale for the continued use."
   - **Assessment**: Requirement acknowledged at high level

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

1. **Insufficient Guidance on Rationale Content**

   - **IEC 62304 Requirement**: Rationale must be "based on the outputs of 4.4"
   - **Expected Rationale Components**:
     - Summary of risk assessment (§4.4.2)
     - Gap analysis results and decisions (§4.4.3)
     - Gap closure activities performed (§4.4.4)
     - Justification for continued use despite any remaining gaps
     - Safety assessment conclusion
   - **D0003329 Coverage**: Generic statement only - no specification of required content
   - **Gap Description**: No guidance on what constitutes adequate rationale
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Recommendation**: Expand §6.1 to specify rationale content:
     - "Rationale shall include: (1) Legacy software version and configuration identification, (2) Summary of risk assessment findings including incident/near-incident review, (3) Gap analysis results showing required vs. available vs. generated deliverables, (4) Safety assessment conclusion demonstrating acceptable risk, (5) Justification for any remaining documentation gaps with risk-benefit analysis, (6) Compliance statement"
     - "Rationale shall reference all §4.4 outputs: risk assessment report, gap analysis, gap closure plan and execution records"

2. **No Specification of Documentation Location**

   - **Gap**: No guidance on where rationale should be documented
   - **D0003329 Reference**: Missing
   - **Risk Level**: Minor
   - **Potential Locations**:
     - Risk Management File
     - Software Development Plan
     - Design History File
     - Standalone Legacy Software Compliance Report
   - **Recommendation**: Add: "Rationale shall be documented in [specify: Risk Management File / Software Development Plan / DHF] with unique document control number. For regulatory submissions, rationale may be required as part of technical documentation."

3. **No Version Control Requirements**
   - **IEC 62304 Emphasis**: "VERSION of the LEGACY SOFTWARE" (capitalized in standard)
   - **Gap**: No elaboration on version identification requirements
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Impact**: Inadequate version control could lead to:
     - Wrong version deployed
     - Confusion over which version was assessed
     - Inability to verify compliance in audits
   - **Recommendation**: Add version requirements:
     - "Version identification shall include: software version number, build identifier, release date, and configuration items (including SOUP)"
     - "Establish configuration baseline for legacy software per D0003329 §6.12 (Configuration Management)"
     - "Any changes to legacy software require updated version and revised rationale"

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

1. **No Review or Approval Process for Rationale**

   - **Gap**: No requirement for review/approval of rationale documentation
   - **D0003329 Reference**: Missing
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Impact**: Inadequate rationale could be accepted without proper scrutiny
   - **Recommendation**: Add approval requirement:
     - "Rationale shall be reviewed by Quality, SME, and Independent Reviewer (per D0003329 §3 Responsibilities)"
     - "Final approval by R&D management required before legacy software continues in production"
     - "Regulatory Affairs review required if rationale will be part of regulatory submission"

2. **No Trigger for Rationale Review/Update**

   - **Gap**: No specification of when rationale must be reviewed or updated
   - **Risk Level**: Significant
   - **Safety Class Impact**: All (A, B, C)
   - **Scenarios Requiring Update**:
     - Legacy software modified
     - New post-market data available
     - Regulatory requirements change
     - Device design incorporating legacy software changes
     - Periodic review (e.g., annual)
   - **Recommendation**: Add review triggers:
     - "Rationale shall be reviewed and updated when: (1) Legacy software is modified, (2) New incident/complaint data emerges, (3) Gap closure activities are completed, (4) Regulatory standards change, (5) At minimum, annually as part of post-market surveillance review"

3. **No Template or Format Guidance**
   - **Gap**: No template or standardized format for rationale documentation
   - **Risk Level**: Minor
   - **Impact**: Inconsistent documentation across projects; difficulty in audits
   - **Recommendation**: Create template: "D000XXXX, Global Template, Legacy Software Rationale for Continued Use" containing sections for: version identification, risk assessment summary, gap analysis results, gap closure summary, safety justification, approval signatures

   - **Impact**: Potential gap in demonstrating device meets user needs when incorporating legacy components

   - **Recommendation**: "Changes to legacy software shall be managed per D0003098 §6.10 (Design Changes). Any change may invalidate §4.4 compliance and require: (1) Impact assessment, (2) Updated risk assessment, (3) Gap analysis review, (4) Revised rationale for continued use."

### Practical Implementation Considerations

1. **Gap Analysis Execution Guidance**

   **Current Gap**: D0003329 §6.1 states "perform a gap analysis" but provides no practical execution guidance.

   **Needed Guidance**:

   - **Tools**: Spreadsheet template, requirements management tool, dedicated software
   - **Approach**:
     1. Identify software safety class (Class A, B, or C)
     2. Create checklist of required deliverables from §5.2, §5.3, §5.7, Clause 7 based on class
     3. Inventory existing legacy documentation (source code, test records, design docs, manuals)
     4. Assess validity of each existing document (accurate, complete, current)
     5. Identify gaps (required but missing or invalid)
     6. Evaluate each gap for risk reduction potential vs. generation effort
     7. Decide generate vs. accept gap with justification
     8. Document findings in gap analysis report
   - **Typical Duration**: 40-80 hours depending on software complexity and available documentation
   - **Resources**: Software engineer (legacy system knowledge), quality engineer, SME for technology domain

   **Recommendation**: Create appendix or supplementary guidance document: "D000XXXX, Guidance, Legacy Software Gap Analysis Execution" with step-by-step process, decision trees, templates, and examples.

2. **Decision Criteria: §4.4 vs. §5-9 Compliance**

   **Current Gap**: No guidance on which path to choose.

   **Decision Framework**:

   **Use §4.4 (Legacy Software Pathway) When**:

   - Software predates IEC 62304 compliance requirement (typically pre-2012 for EU, varies by jurisdiction)
   - Insufficient original development documentation exists
   - Software has established field history demonstrating safety
   - Cost to retrofit full §5-9 compliance is prohibitive relative to risk
   - Software is in late lifecycle with limited future development planned

   **Use §5-9 (Full Compliance) When**:

   - Major software changes planned that would require extensive rework anyway
   - Software critical to safety (Class C) with limited field history
   - Original development documentation is available and valid
   - Long product lifecycle expected with ongoing development
   - Competitive or market advantage from demonstrating full compliance

   **Recommendation**: Add decision criteria to D0003329 §6.1 and create decision tree tool.

3. **Resource and Timeline Planning**

   **Current Gap**: No guidance on effort required for legacy software compliance.

   **Typical Resource Requirements**:

   | Activity                         | Class A         | Class B         | Class C           |
   | -------------------------------- | --------------- | --------------- | ----------------- |
   | Risk Assessment (§4.4.2)         | 20-40 hrs       | 40-80 hrs       | 80-160 hrs        |
   | Gap Analysis (§4.4.3)            | 20-40 hrs       | 40-80 hrs       | 80-120 hrs        |
   | Gap Closure Plan (§4.4.4)        | 10-20 hrs       | 20-40 hrs       | 40-60 hrs         |
   | Deliverable Generation           | 40-120 hrs      | 120-400 hrs     | 400-1000+ hrs     |
   | System Testing                   | 40-80 hrs       | 80-200 hrs      | 200-400 hrs       |
   | Rationale Documentation (§4.4.5) | 10-20 hrs       | 20-40 hrs       | 40-80 hrs         |
   | **Total**                        | **140-320 hrs** | **320-840 hrs** | **840-1820+ hrs** |

   **Timeline**: 3-6 months typical for Class B, 6-12 months for Class C (depending on complexity and available resources)

   **Recommendation**: Add resource planning guidance to §6.1 or §6.2 (Software Development Planning). Include note that timeline depends on software complexity, available documentation, and whether changes are needed.

4. **Templates and Checklists**

   **Current Gap**: No templates referenced in D0003329 §6.1.

   **Recommended Templates/Tools**:

   - **Gap Analysis Template**: Structured form capturing required deliverables, existing artifacts, validity assessment, gaps, decisions
   - **Risk Assessment Checklist**: Structured evaluation of §4.4.2.a feedback sources and §4.4.2.b risk aspects
   - **Gap Closure Plan Template**: Standard format for documenting deliverables to generate, methods, timeline, resources
   - **Rationale Document Template**: Standardized format for §4.4.5 documentation
   - **Legacy Software Deliverable Checklist**: Quick reference showing required deliverables by safety class

   **Recommendation**: Develop template suite with document control numbers (D000XXXX series). Reference templates in D0003329 §6.1.

### Cross-Document Consistency

1. **Terminology Alignment**

   **Assessment**: Generally consistent terminology between D0003329 and D0003098.

   **Minor Inconsistencies**:

   - D0003329 uses "Software Safety Classification" while D0003098 uses "Software Safety Class" - acceptable variation
   - D0003329 references "risk management file" while D0003098 §6.3 uses "Risk Analysis" and "Risk Management" interchangeably - recommend standardization

   **Recommendation**: Conduct terminology audit and publish glossary appendix (or enhance D0004089 Global Glossary) specifically for software terms used across both documents.

2. **Risk Management Process Alignment**

   **D0003329 §6.11 vs. D0003098 Risk References**:

   - D0003329 §6.11 addresses software-specific risk management per IEC 62304 Clause 7
   - D0003098 references risk analysis throughout design phases per ISO 14971
   - **Gap**: D0003329 §6.1 (Legacy Software) does not explicitly link §4.4.2 risk assessment to §6.11 process
   - **Recommendation**: Add to D0003329 §6.1: "Legacy software risk assessment per §4.4.2 shall be performed and documented according to §6.11 (Software-Specific Elements of Risk Management Process) and integrated into overall device risk management per ISO 14971 and D0003098."

3. **Problem Resolution Process Integration**

   **D0003329 §6.13 vs. Legacy Software**:

   - D0003329 §6.13 describes Software Problem Resolution Process per BS EN 62304 Clause 9
   - IEC 62304 §4.4.4.b requires gap closure plan address problem resolution
   - **Gap**: No cross-reference from §6.1 to §6.13
   - **Recommendation**: Add to D0003329 §6.1: "Problems identified during legacy software gap analysis, gap closure activities, or testing shall be handled per §6.13 (Software Problem Resolution Process)."

4. **Configuration Management Alignment**

   **D0003329 §6.12 vs. Legacy Software**:

   - D0003329 §6.12 describes Software Configuration Management per BS EN 62304 Clause 8
   - IEC 62304 §4.4.4.c requires changes to legacy software follow Clause 6 (Maintenance), which depends on configuration management
   - §4.4.5 requires version documentation
   - **Gap**: No cross-reference from §6.1 to §6.12
   - **Recommendation**: Add to D0003329 §6.1: "Legacy software shall be placed under configuration management per §6.12. Version baseline shall be established and documented as part of §4.4.5 rationale. Any changes shall follow configuration management and maintenance processes (§6.10, §6.12)."

5. **Testing Requirements Consistency**

   **D0003329 §6.8 (Software System Testing) vs. §4.4.3 Minimum Deliverable**:

   - §6.8 describes system testing per BS EN 62304 §5.7
   - IEC 62304 §4.4.3.c requires minimum deliverable of system test records (§5.7.5)
   - **Gap**: §6.1 does not reference §6.8 for testing requirements
   - **Recommendation**: Add to D0003329 §6.1: "Legacy software system testing (minimum required deliverable) shall be performed per §6.8 (Software System Testing) and §5.7 of BS EN 62304. Test records shall be documented per §5.7.5."

6. **Software Development Planning Integration**

   **D0003329 §6.2 vs. §6.1**:

   - §6.2 describes Software Development Plan per BS EN 62304 §5.1
   - IEC 62304 §4.4.4 Note suggests gap closure plan can be included in maintenance plan (§6.1 of standard, §6.10 of D0003329)
   - **Gap**: No guidance on whether legacy software should be addressed in Software Development Plan
   - **Recommendation**: Add to D0003329 §6.2: "For projects incorporating legacy software, Software Development Plan shall address: (1) Use of §4.4 compliance pathway, (2) Reference to gap analysis, (3) Reference to gap closure plan, (4) Integration strategy for legacy components." Also add to §6.1: "Legacy software gap closure plan may be standalone or incorporated into Software Development Plan (§6.2) or Software Maintenance Plan (§6.10)."

## Conclusion

D0003329 Rev 03 Section 6.1 establishes awareness of IEC 62304 Clause 4.4 requirements and provides high-level direction for legacy software compliance. However, the work instruction falls short of providing sufficient procedural detail, process integration, and practical implementation guidance needed for consistent, auditable execution.

- Risk management activities specification (§4.4.2)

- Decision criteria and pathway selection guidance
- Template and tool development
- Cross-document integration with D0003098
- Rationale content and approval requirements

Addressing these gaps will strengthen regulatory compliance, improve audit preparedness, reduce variability in execution, and ultimately enhance the safety and quality of medical devices incorporating legacy software.
