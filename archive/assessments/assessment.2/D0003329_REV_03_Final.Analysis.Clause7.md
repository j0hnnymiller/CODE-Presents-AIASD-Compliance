# IEC 62304 Clause 7 (Software Risk Management) Compliance Assessment

## D0003329 Rev 03 - Software Risk Management Process

**Assessment Date**: December 16, 2025
**Assessor**: Systematic Compliance Analysis
**Standard**: BS EN 62304:2006+A1:2015 (IEC 62304)
**Scope**: Clause 7 - Software Risk Management Process

---

## Executive Summary

D0003329 Rev 03 §6.11 provides **partial coverage** of IEC 62304 Clause 7 requirements with significant gaps in procedural detail and explicit requirement mapping. The work instruction establishes foundational concepts but lacks the systematic, step-by-step implementation guidance necessary for consistent regulatory compliance.

**Compliance Status**: 5 of 11 requirements compliant (45%), 6 partial/missing (55%)

**Key Strengths**:

- Integration with D0003103 (Risk Management Procedure) established
- Cybersecurity risk management addressed (D0029257 reference)
- Software safety classification properly linked to risk control measures
- Traceability requirements documented for Class B/C

**Critical Gaps**:

- Missing explicit procedures for identifying software items contributing to hazards (§7.1.1)
- No detailed methodology for identifying potential causes (§7.1.2)
- SOUP anomaly evaluation process not specified (§7.1.3)
- Risk control measure definition process lacks detail (§7.2.1)
- Verification procedures for risk control measures insufficiently detailed (§7.3.1)
- Change impact analysis procedures incomplete (§7.4.2, §7.4.3)

## Section 7.1: Analysis of Software Contributing to Hazardous Situations

### 7.1.1: Identify SOFTWARE ITEMS that could contribute to a hazardous situation

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "The MANUFACTURER shall identify SOFTWARE ITEMS that could contribute to a hazardous situation identified in the MEDICAL DEVICE RISK ANALYSIS ACTIVITY of ISO 14971 (see 4.2). [Class B, C]"

**D0003329 Coverage**: §6.11, paragraph 1

**Compliant Elements** ✅

- **Integration with ISO 14971**: "Risk analysis of products shall be in accordance with the overall project Risk Management Plan"
- **Scope identification**: "This shall identify software items that may contribute to a hazardous situation involving software, including SOUP"
- **Cross-reference to D0003103**: Establishes linkage to overall risk management process

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

- **Missing detailed methodology**: No explicit procedure for **how** to identify software items
- **No guidance on granularity**: What constitutes a "software item" for hazard analysis purposes?
- **Unclear identification criteria**: When is a software item considered to "contribute" to a hazardous situation?

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

- **No systematic identification process**: Lacks step-by-step procedure (e.g., "Review system hazards → Trace to software functions → Identify contributing software items")
- **Missing examples**: No illustrative examples of software items that contribute to hazards vs. those that don't
- **Incomplete SOUP integration**: While SOUP is mentioned, no specific guidance on identifying SOUP items contributing to hazards
- **No template/form reference**: No structured documentation format specified

**Gap Analysis**:
The requirement is acknowledged in principle but lacks the procedural depth needed for consistent implementation. Teams need explicit guidance on:

1. How to systematically review system-level hazards
2. How to trace hazards to specific software items
3. How to determine the scope/granularity of analysis
4. How to document the analysis

**Regulatory Risk Level**: **CRITICAL**
**Safety Class Impact**: B, C
**Remediation Priority**: **HIGH**

---

### 7.1.2: Identify potential causes of contribution to a hazardous situation

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "The MANUFACTURER shall identify potential causes of the SOFTWARE ITEM identified above contributing to a hazardous situation. The MANUFACTURER shall consider potential causes including, as appropriate:
> a) incorrect or incomplete specification of functionality;
> b) software defects in the identified SOFTWARE ITEM functionality;
> c) failure or unexpected results from SOUP;
> d) hardware failures or other software defects that could result in unpredictable software operation; and
> e) reasonably foreseeable misuse. [Class B, C]"

**D0003329 Coverage**: §6.11, implicit in paragraph 1

**Compliant Elements** ✅

- **SOUP consideration**: "including SOUP" acknowledges SOUP as potential cause
- **Integration with risk management**: Reference to overall Risk Management Plan suggests potential cause analysis is part of that process

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

- **Implicit coverage only**: The five specific cause categories (a-e) are not explicitly addressed
- **Missing procedural guidance**: No methodology for systematically identifying potential causes
- **Incomplete documentation requirements**: No specification of how potential causes should be documented

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

- **No explicit mapping to IEC 62304 §7.1.2(a)-(e)**: The five cause categories are not mentioned
  - (a) Incorrect/incomplete specification
  - (b) Software defects in identified item
  - (c) SOUP failure/unexpected results
  - (d) Hardware failures causing unpredictable operation
  - (e) Reasonably foreseeable misuse
- **Missing FMEA/FMECA guidance**: No reference to failure modes analysis techniques appropriate for software
- **No examples or case studies**: Lacks practical guidance on what constitutes each type of potential cause
- **Insufficient SOUP analysis**: While SOUP is mentioned in §7.1.1, there's no guidance on analyzing SOUP-specific failure modes
- **Missing cybersecurity threat modeling**: While D0029257 is referenced, integration with software cause analysis is not explicit

**Gap Analysis**:
This is a critical procedural gap. D0003329 relies entirely on D0003103 (Risk Management Procedure) without specifying software-specific cause analysis requirements. The five categories in IEC 62304 §7.1.2 are specifically tailored to software hazard analysis and must be explicitly addressed.

**Regulatory Risk Level**: **CRITICAL**
**Safety Class Impact**: B, C
**Remediation Priority**: **HIGH**

**Recommended Action**:
Add explicit subsection to §6.11 requiring identification of potential causes using the five IEC 62304 categories. Reference appropriate analysis techniques (e.g., software FMEA, fault tree analysis, use case misuse analysis).

### 7.1.3: EVALUATE published SOUP ANOMALY lists

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "If failure or unexpected results from SOUP is a potential cause of the SOFTWARE ITEM contributing to a hazardous situation, the MANUFACTURER shall EVALUATE as a minimum any ANOMALY list published by the supplier of the SOUP item relevant to the VERSION of the SOUP item used in the MEDICAL DEVICE to determine if any of the known ANOMALIES result in a sequence of events that could result in a hazardous situation. [Class B, C]"

**D0003329 Coverage**: Not explicitly addressed in §6.11

**Compliant Elements** ✅

- **SOUP identification required**: §6.11 mentions "including SOUP" in hazard identification
- **SOUP documentation**: §6.12 (Configuration Management) requires SOUP documentation per §8.1.2

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

- **General SOUP awareness**: Work instruction acknowledges SOUP as risk factor but doesn't specify anomaly evaluation

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

- **No explicit SOUP anomaly evaluation requirement**: §6.11 does not mention evaluating published SOUP anomaly lists
- **Missing procedure for anomaly list evaluation**: No guidance on:
  - Where to find SOUP anomaly lists (e.g., CVE databases, vendor security bulletins, release notes)
  - How frequently to evaluate (initial selection, periodic review, version updates)
  - How to assess if anomaly could result in hazardous situation
  - How to document evaluation results
- **No cybersecurity vulnerability integration**: While D0029257 is referenced for cybersecurity risks, the connection to SOUP anomaly evaluation is not explicit
- **Missing version-specific requirements**: No requirement to evaluate anomaly lists for the **specific VERSION** of SOUP used
- **No ongoing monitoring process**: No requirement for periodic re-evaluation of SOUP anomaly lists during product lifecycle
- **Insufficient documentation requirements**: No specification of where/how SOUP anomaly evaluations should be documented

**Gap Analysis**:
This is a significant compliance gap. SOUP anomaly evaluation is a specific, measurable requirement in IEC 62304 and is critical for software safety. Modern medical devices rely heavily on SOUP (operating systems, libraries, frameworks), making this evaluation essential for risk management.

The cybersecurity reference (D0029257) may partially address this for security vulnerabilities, but the requirement applies to **all** SOUP anomalies that could contribute to hazardous situations, not just security issues.

**Regulatory Risk Level**: **CRITICAL**
**Safety Class Impact**: B, C
**Remediation Priority**: **HIGH**

**Recommended Action**:
Add explicit requirement to §6.11:

- "For SOUP items identified as contributing to hazardous situations, the manufacturer shall evaluate published anomaly lists (including CVE databases, vendor security bulletins, and release notes) for the specific version used."
- "Evaluation shall determine if known anomalies could result in a sequence of events leading to a hazardous situation."
- "Evaluations shall be documented in the Risk Management File and repeated when SOUP versions are updated or new anomalies are published."
- Cross-reference to D0029257 for cybersecurity-specific vulnerability evaluation procedures.

### 7.1.4: Document potential causes

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "The MANUFACTURER shall document in the RISK MANAGEMENT FILE potential causes of the SOFTWARE ITEM contributing to a hazardous situation (see ISO 14971). [Class B, C]"

**D0003329 Coverage**: §6.11, implicit in D0003103 reference

**Compliant Elements** ✅

- **Risk Management File reference**: "any new sequence of events that could result in a hazardous situation shall be identified in the Risk Management File – See D0003103 Global Procedure, Risk Management"
- **Integration with ISO 14971**: Clear connection established between software risk analysis and overall risk management documentation

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

- **Implicit documentation requirement**: While Risk Management File is mentioned, the specific requirement to document **potential causes** is not explicit
- **Documentation scope unclear**: Does not explicitly state that potential causes identified in §7.1.2 analysis must be documented

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

- **No explicit linkage to §7.1.2 analysis**: The work instruction should explicitly state that the potential causes identified per §7.1.2 (a-e categories) must be documented
- **Missing documentation format guidance**: No specification of how potential causes should be structured in the Risk Management File
- **No template reference**: No reference to a structured format or template for documenting software-specific potential causes
- **Insufficient traceability guidance**: While traceability is addressed later (§6.11 for Class B/C), the connection to potential cause documentation is not made explicit here

**Gap Analysis**:
The requirement is partially met through the D0003103 reference, but lacks explicit procedural guidance. Since §7.1.2 analysis (identifying potential causes) is itself inadequately addressed in D0003329, the documentation of those causes is also problematic.

The work instruction would benefit from explicit statement: "Potential causes identified per §7.1.2 shall be documented in the Risk Management File for each software item contributing to a hazardous situation."

**Regulatory Risk Level**: **SIGNIFICANT**
**Safety Class Impact**: B, C
**Remediation Priority**: **MEDIUM**

**Recommended Action**:
Strengthen §6.11 with explicit documentation requirement:

- "All potential causes identified for software items contributing to hazardous situations shall be documented in the Risk Management File."
- "Documentation shall include the five cause categories specified in IEC 62304 §7.1.2."
- Reference appropriate section of D0003103 or provide template for software hazard cause documentation.

## Section 7.2: Risk Control Measures

### 7.2.1: Define RISK CONTROL measures

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "For each case documented in the RISK MANAGEMENT FILE where a SOFTWARE ITEM could contribute to a HAZARDOUS SITUATION, the MANUFACTURER shall define and document RISK CONTROL measures in accordance with ISO 14971. [Class B, C]"

**D0003329 Coverage**: §6.11, paragraphs 2-3

**Compliant Elements** ✅

- **ISO 14971 integration**: Clear reference to overall Risk Management Plan and D0003103 establishes ISO 14971 compliance framework
- **Documentation requirement**: "any new sequence of events that could result in a hazardous situation shall be identified in the Risk Management File"
- **Risk control measure awareness**: The work instruction acknowledges that risk control measures must be defined

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

- **Implicit definition requirement**: The requirement to **define** risk control measures is implied through ISO 14971 integration but not explicitly stated
- **Missing procedural guidance**: No explicit procedure for defining risk control measures for software hazards
- **Scope ambiguity**: Does not explicitly state requirement applies to "**each case**" where software contributes to hazardous situation

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

- **No explicit "define and document" requirement**: §6.11 should explicitly state: "For each software item contributing to a hazardous situation, risk control measures shall be defined and documented in the Risk Management File per ISO 14971"
- **Missing risk control hierarchy guidance**: No reference to ISO 14971 risk control hierarchy (inherently safe design > protective measures > information for safety)
- **Insufficient software-specific guidance**: While D0003103 addresses general risk control, software-specific considerations are not detailed
- **No examples of software risk controls**: Lacks illustrative examples (e.g., input validation, fail-safe states, redundancy, error handling)
- **Missing effectiveness criteria**: No guidance on how to determine if a proposed risk control measure is adequate

**Gap Analysis**:
The requirement is partially met through integration with D0003103 (ISO 14971), but D0003329 should provide explicit software-specific guidance. Teams need clear direction that:

1. Risk control measures must be defined for **every** software item contributing to hazards
2. Definition must follow ISO 14971 risk control hierarchy
3. Software-specific risk control strategies should be considered
4. Documentation must be in Risk Management File

**Regulatory Risk Level**: **SIGNIFICANT**
**Safety Class Impact**: B, C
**Remediation Priority**: **MEDIUM**

**Recommended Action**:
Add explicit requirement to §6.11:

- "For each software item that could contribute to a hazardous situation (identified per §7.1), risk control measures shall be defined and documented in the Risk Management File in accordance with ISO 14971."
- "Risk control measures shall follow the ISO 14971 hierarchy: inherently safe design, protective measures, information for safety."
- Provide examples of software-specific risk control strategies.

### 7.2.2: RISK CONTROL measures implemented in software

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "If a RISK CONTROL measure is implemented as part of the functions of a SOFTWARE ITEM, the MANUFACTURER shall:
> a) include the RISK CONTROL measure in the software requirements;
> b) assign to each SOFTWARE ITEM that contributes to the implementation of a RISK CONTROL measure a software safety class based on the RISK that the RISK CONTROL measure is controlling (see 4.3 a)); and
> c) develop the SOFTWARE ITEM in accordance with Clause 5. [Class B, C]"

**D0003329 Coverage**: §6.11, paragraphs 2-3

**Compliant Elements** ✅

- **Requirement (a) - Include in software requirements**: "If a risk control measure is implemented in the software, it shall be included in the Software Requirements" (§6.11, paragraph 2) - **FULLY COMPLIANT**
- **Requirement (b) - Assign safety class**: "A software safety class shall be assigned to software Items based on the possible effects of the risk that the risk control measure is controlling" (§6.11, paragraph 3) - **FULLY COMPLIANT**
- **Requirement (c) - Develop per Clause 5**: Implicit through overall D0003329 structure covering all Clause 5 activities - **COMPLIANT**

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

- **Integration with development process**: While Clause 5 development is covered elsewhere in D0003329, the explicit connection to risk control measure implementation could be clearer

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

- **No explicit reference to IEC 62304 §4.3(a)**: While safety classification is required, the specific reference to §4.3(a) determination methodology is not cited
- **Missing traceability requirement**: Should explicitly state that risk control measures in software requirements must trace back to hazards in Risk Management File
- **Insufficient guidance on safety class assignment methodology**: While requirement is stated, the **process** for determining appropriate safety class based on controlled risk is not detailed
- **No verification requirement**: Should explicitly state that software items implementing risk controls require verification per §7.3

**Gap Analysis**:
This is the **best-covered** requirement in §6.11, with explicit addressing of all three sub-requirements (a, b, c). However, procedural detail could be enhanced to ensure consistent implementation.

The connection between:

- Hazard in Risk Management File → Risk Control Measure → Software Requirement → Software Design → Verification
  could be more explicitly documented.

**Regulatory Risk Level**: **MINOR**
**Safety Class Impact**: B, C
**Remediation Priority**: **LOW**

**Recommended Action**:
Enhance §6.11 with explicit traceability requirement:

- "Software requirements implementing risk control measures shall be traced to the corresponding hazards in the Risk Management File."
- "Safety class assignment shall follow the methodology in Appendix 10.1 (Software Safety Classification) with consideration of the risk level being controlled."
- "Software items implementing risk control measures shall be verified per §7.3."

## Section 7.3: Verification of Risk Control Measures

### 7.3.1: Verify RISK CONTROL measures

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "The implementation of each RISK CONTROL measure documented in 7.2 shall be VERIFIED, and this VERIFICATION shall be documented. The MANUFACTURER shall review the RISK CONTROL measure and determine if it could result in a new HAZARDOUS SITUATION. [Class B, C]"

**D0003329 Coverage**: §6.11, implicit through §6.8 (Software System Testing)

**Compliant Elements** ✅

- **General verification process**: §6.8 "Software System Testing" establishes verification framework
- **Test documentation**: §6.8 requires documentation of verification activities
- **D0017982 reference**: Global Template, Software Verification Protocol provides structured verification approach

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

- **Risk control verification mentioned indirectly**: Traceability requirements (§6.11 paragraph 5) imply verification of risk control measures
- **Testing requirements exist but lack risk-specific focus**: §6.8 covers testing but doesn't explicitly call out risk control measure verification as a specific category

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

- **No explicit requirement in §6.11**: Section 6.11 (Risk Management) does not explicitly state: "Implementation of each risk control measure shall be verified"
- **Missing verification methodology**: No guidance on **how** to verify risk control measures (e.g., inspection, analysis, test)
- **Insufficient documentation requirements**: While verification documentation is required generally (§6.8), risk control measure-specific documentation requirements are not detailed
- **No explicit "new hazard" review requirement**: The critical requirement to "determine if [risk control measure] could result in a new HAZARDOUS SITUATION" is **NOT** mentioned in §6.11
  - This is a key IEC 62304 requirement - risk controls themselves can introduce new hazards
  - Must assess if the implementation of the risk control creates new risks
- **Missing link between §6.11 and §6.8**: No explicit cross-reference requiring risk control measures to be included in verification protocols
- **No template guidance**: D0017982 (Verification Protocol) may not explicitly prompt for risk control measure verification

**Gap Analysis**:
This is a **significant procedural gap**. While D0003329 has a robust verification process (§6.8), it does not explicitly connect risk control measure verification to that process within §6.11.

Most critically, the requirement to review risk control measures for **new hazards** is completely absent. This is a vital safety requirement - risk controls can inadvertently introduce new failure modes.

**Regulatory Risk Level**: **CRITICAL**
**Safety Class Impact**: B, C
**Remediation Priority**: **HIGH**

**Recommended Action**:
Add explicit requirements to §6.11:

1. "The implementation of each risk control measure shall be verified. Verification shall be documented in the Risk Management File and software verification documentation."
2. "Each risk control measure shall be reviewed to determine if its implementation could result in a new hazardous situation. Any new hazards shall be addressed through the risk management process per D0003103."
3. "Risk control measure verification shall be included in Software Verification Protocols (D0017982)."
4. Cross-reference §6.8 (Software System Testing) for verification execution procedures.

### 7.3.3: Document TRACEABILITY

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "The MANUFACTURER shall document TRACEABILITY of software HAZARDS as appropriate:
> a) from the hazardous situation to the SOFTWARE ITEM;
> b) from the SOFTWARE ITEM to the specific software cause;
> c) from the software cause to the RISK CONTROL measure; and
> d) from the RISK CONTROL measure to the VERIFICATION of the RISK CONTROL measure. [Class B, C]"

**D0003329 Coverage**: §6.11, paragraph 5 (For Class B and C Software)

**Compliant Elements** ✅

- **Explicit traceability requirement**: "Traceability of software hazards shall be documented as appropriate from the hazardous situation to the software item to the specific software cause to the risk control measure to the verification of the risk control measure" - **FULLY COMPLIANT**
- **Complete traceability chain**: All four linkages (a-d) are explicitly mentioned
- **Class B/C distinction**: Correctly scoped to Class B and C software per standard
- **"As appropriate" qualifier**: Includes IEC 62304's "as appropriate" language, allowing flexibility based on risk

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

- **Documentation method not specified**: While traceability is required, the **how** (e.g., traceability matrix, database, tool) is not specified
- **Template reference**: D0017985 (Software Traceability Matrix) is listed in §5 (Cited Documents) but not explicitly referenced in §6.11

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

- **No explicit reference to D0017985**: §6.11 should explicitly reference D0017985, Global Template, Software Traceability Matrix as the documentation vehicle
- **Missing guidance on "as appropriate"**: No explanation of when traceability is "appropriate" vs. when it might be reduced (presumably based on risk level)
- **Insufficient integration with other processes**:
  - No cross-reference to §6.3 (Software Requirements) for requirement-level traceability
  - No cross-reference to §6.8 (Software System Testing) for verification traceability
- **No example or visual**: A simple diagram or example showing the traceability flow would significantly enhance understanding

**Gap Analysis**:
This requirement has **excellent** textual coverage - the language in D0003329 §6.11 paragraph 5 directly mirrors IEC 62304 §7.3.3. However, it lacks procedural implementation details that would help teams execute the requirement.

The traceability requirement is one of the most critical for regulatory compliance and audit preparation. While the requirement is stated, the **mechanism** for documentation needs explicit specification.

**Regulatory Risk Level**: **MINOR** (requirement stated, but procedural gap exists)
**Safety Class Impact**: B, C
**Remediation Priority**: **LOW-MEDIUM**

**Recommended Action**:
Enhance §6.11 paragraph 5:

1. Explicitly reference D0017985 (Software Traceability Matrix): "Traceability shall be documented using D0017985, Global Template, Software Traceability Matrix, or equivalent structured format."
2. Add guidance on "as appropriate": "The extent of traceability documentation shall be commensurate with the software safety class and risk level. Higher risks require more detailed traceability."
3. Add visual diagram showing traceability flow: Hazardous Situation → Software Item → Software Cause → Risk Control → Verification
4. Cross-reference supporting templates (D0017982 for verification, D0004169 for requirements)

## Section 7.4: Risk Management of Software Changes

### 7.4.1: Analyze changes to MEDICAL DEVICE SOFTWARE with respect to SAFETY

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "The MANUFACTURER shall analyse changes to the MEDICAL DEVICE SOFTWARE (including SOUP) to determine whether:
> a) additional potential causes are introduced contributing to a hazardous situation; and
> b) additional software RISK CONTROL measures are required. [Class A, B, C]"

**D0003329 Coverage**: §6.11, not explicitly addressed; §6.10 (Software Maintenance) provides partial coverage

**Compliant Elements** ✅

- **Change management exists**: §6.10 (Software Maintenance) and §6.13 (Software Problem Resolution Process) establish change management framework
- **Safety evaluation implicit**: §6.13 requires that "all anomalies identified shall be investigated, evaluated for safety"
- **Risk management integration**: §6.10 references "risk assessment" for maintenance changes

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

- **§6.13 safety evaluation**: "All anomalies identified shall be investigated, evaluated for safety, and will result in a change request or rationale for taking no action"
- **General safety consideration**: Safety is mentioned in change processes but not with the specific focus required by §7.4.1

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

- **§6.11 does not address change analysis**: Section 6.11 (Risk Management) should include explicit requirements for risk analysis of software changes, but it does not
- **Missing explicit §7.4.1(a) analysis**: No requirement to determine if changes "introduce additional potential causes contributing to a hazardous situation"
- **Missing explicit §7.4.1(b) analysis**: No requirement to determine if "additional software risk control measures are required"
- **SOUP change analysis insufficient**: While §7.4.1 explicitly includes "(including SOUP)", D0003329 does not explicitly require safety analysis when SOUP versions are updated
- **No change-specific hazard analysis procedure**: Missing methodology for:
  - Reviewing changed code/functionality for new hazard contributions
  - Assessing impact on existing hazard analysis
  - Determining if new potential causes are introduced
  - Identifying need for new risk control measures
- **Applies to ALL classes**: IEC 62304 §7.4.1 applies to **Class A, B, C** - this broader applicability is not reflected in §6.11 structure
- **No cross-reference between §6.11 and §6.10/§6.13**: Risk management of changes should be explicitly covered in the risk management section with clear linkage to maintenance/problem resolution processes

**Gap Analysis**:
This is a **critical procedural gap**. IEC 62304 §7.4 recognizes that software changes are a major source of new risks and requires specific safety analysis of all changes. D0003329 handles changes through §6.10 (Maintenance) and §6.13 (Problem Resolution) but does not integrate change-specific risk analysis into §6.11 (Risk Management).

The requirement applies to **all safety classes** (A, B, C), not just B and C, reflecting the reality that even Class A software changes can introduce new hazards.

**Regulatory Risk Level**: **CRITICAL**
**Safety Class Impact**: A, B, C (ALL classes)
**Remediation Priority**: **HIGH**

**Recommended Action**:
Add new paragraph to §6.11:

1. "All changes to medical device software, including SOUP updates, shall be analyzed for safety impact to determine:
   a) Whether the change introduces additional potential causes contributing to a hazardous situation;
   b) Whether additional risk control measures are required."
2. "This analysis applies to all software safety classes (A, B, C) and shall be documented in the Risk Management File."
3. "Safety analysis of changes shall be integrated with the software maintenance process (§6.10) and software problem resolution process (§6.13)."
4. Cross-reference D0003103 for change risk assessment procedures.

### 7.4.2: Analyze impact of software changes on existing RISK CONTROL measures

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "The MANUFACTURER shall analyse changes to the software, including changes to SOUP, to determine whether the software modification could interfere with existing RISK CONTROL measures. [Class B, C]"

**D0003329 Coverage**: §6.11, not explicitly addressed; §6.13 provides minimal coverage

**Compliant Elements** ✅

- **General change verification**: §6.13 requires "any actions taken to resolve the anomaly shall be verified and ensure the change did not result in additional anomalies"
- **Regression consideration**: Implicit through verification requirement that changes don't introduce new problems

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

- **Verification of changes**: §6.13 requires verification of changes, which could include checking impact on risk controls
- **Regression testing**: §6.8 (Software System Testing) mentions regression testing, which could include risk control measure verification

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

- **§6.11 does not address this requirement**: No explicit requirement to analyze change impact on existing risk control measures
- **Missing explicit interference analysis**: No requirement to specifically check if changes "interfere with" or compromise existing risk controls
- **SOUP update impact not addressed**: While §7.4.2 explicitly includes "changes to SOUP", D0003329 does not require analyzing SOUP updates for impact on risk controls
- **No procedure for impact analysis**: Missing methodology for:
  - Identifying which existing risk control measures could be affected by a change
  - Assessing whether the change compromises the effectiveness of risk controls
  - Determining if risk controls need to be re-verified after changes
  - Documenting the impact analysis
- **Missing cross-reference to risk control inventory**: No requirement to maintain an inventory of risk control measures that can be checked against changes
- **Insufficient traceability utilization**: While D0017985 (Traceability Matrix) exists, there's no explicit requirement to use it for identifying risk controls affected by changes
- **No explicit re-verification trigger**: Should state that changes affecting risk controls trigger re-verification per §7.3.1

**Gap Analysis**:
This is a **critical compliance gap**. Software changes are a primary cause of regression issues, and risk control measures are often unintentionally compromised by well-intentioned changes. IEC 62304 specifically requires analyzing whether changes **interfere with** existing risk controls.

D0003329 has general verification and regression testing requirements, but does not explicitly require the risk control interference analysis specified in §7.4.2.

**Regulatory Risk Level**: **CRITICAL**
**Safety Class Impact**: B, C
**Remediation Priority**: **HIGH**

**Recommended Action**:
Add explicit requirement to §6.11:

1. "All software changes, including SOUP updates, shall be analyzed to determine whether the modification could interfere with or compromise existing risk control measures (Class B, C)."
2. "The analysis shall:
   - Identify existing risk control measures potentially affected by the change
   - Assess whether the change could reduce the effectiveness of risk controls
   - Document the analysis in the Risk Management File
   - Trigger re-verification of affected risk control measures per §7.3.1"
3. "The software traceability matrix (D0017985) shall be used to identify risk control measures affected by changes."
4. Cross-reference §6.13 (Problem Resolution) and §6.10 (Maintenance) for change execution procedures.

### 7.4.3: Perform RISK MANAGEMENT ACTIVITIES based on analyses

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "The MANUFACTURER shall perform relevant RISK MANAGEMENT ACTIVITIES defined in 7.1, 7.2 and 7.3 based on these analyses. [Class B, C]"

**D0003329 Coverage**: §6.11, not explicitly addressed

**Compliant Elements** ✅

- **Risk management integration exists**: §6.11 references D0003103 (Global Procedure, Risk Management), which would include risk management activities
- **General risk assessment for changes**: §6.10 (Software Maintenance) mentions "risk assessment" for maintenance activities

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

- **Implicit through D0003103**: The overall risk management process would theoretically include performing risk activities based on change analyses
- **Problem resolution process**: §6.13 requires "evaluated for safety" which implies risk management activities

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

- **No explicit requirement**: §6.11 does not state that risk management activities (§7.1, 7.2, 7.3) must be performed based on change analyses
- **Missing procedural linkage**: No explicit connection between:
  - Change analysis findings (§7.4.1, §7.4.2) →
  - Triggering risk management activities (§7.1, 7.2, 7.3)
- **No decision criteria**: Missing guidance on when change analysis results require:
  - Re-performing hazard analysis (§7.1)
  - Defining new risk control measures (§7.2)
  - Re-verifying existing risk control measures (§7.3)
- **Insufficient documentation requirements**: No specification of how risk management activities triggered by changes should be documented
- **Missing examples**: No illustrative examples of change scenarios and corresponding required risk activities, such as:
  - "If §7.4.1 analysis identifies new potential causes → perform §7.1 hazard analysis"
  - "If §7.4.2 analysis identifies interference with risk controls → perform §7.3 re-verification"
- **No cross-reference to traceability**: Should reference D0017985 (Traceability Matrix) to track updated risk management activities

**Gap Analysis**:
This requirement is the **integrating requirement** that closes the loop on software change risk management. It ensures that when changes introduce new hazards (§7.4.1) or affect existing risk controls (§7.4.2), the manufacturer actually performs the necessary risk management activities.

D0003329 lacks explicit guidance on this critical step. While general risk management processes exist (D0003103), the specific trigger mechanism from change analysis to risk activities is not documented.

**Regulatory Risk Level**: **CRITICAL**
**Safety Class Impact**: B, C
**Remediation Priority**: **HIGH**

**Recommended Action**:
Add explicit requirement to §6.11:

1. "Based on the safety analyses of software changes (§7.4.1 and §7.4.2), relevant risk management activities shall be performed as appropriate:
   - If new hazards or potential causes are identified → perform hazard analysis per §7.1
   - If new risk control measures are needed → define and implement per §7.2
   - If existing risk control measures are affected → re-verify per §7.3"
2. "Risk management activities triggered by changes shall be documented in the Risk Management File."
3. "The software traceability matrix (D0017985) shall be updated to reflect new or modified risk management elements resulting from changes."
4. "For Class B and C software, change-triggered risk management activities shall follow the same rigor as initial risk management activities."

## Remediation Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (Critical Priority)

#### 1. Add Explicit Change Risk Management (§7.4) Requirements

**Target**: Addresses gaps #3, #4, #5

**Action**: Add new subsection to §6.11:

> **6.11.x Risk Management of Software Changes**
>
> All changes to medical device software, including SOUP updates, shall be analyzed for safety impact:
>
> a) **Safety Analysis** (Class A, B, C): Determine whether changes introduce:
>
> - Additional potential causes contributing to hazardous situations
> - Need for additional software risk control measures
>
> b) **Impact on Existing Risk Controls** (Class B, C): Analyze whether changes could interfere with or compromise existing risk control measures
>
> c) **Risk Management Activities**: Based on analyses (a) and (b), perform relevant risk management activities:
>
> - If new hazards/causes identified → perform hazard analysis per §7.1
> - If new risk controls needed → define and implement per §7.2
> - If existing risk controls affected → re-verify per §7.3
>
> d) **Documentation**: All change-related risk analyses and resulting risk management activities shall be documented in the Risk Management File.
>
> e) **Integration**: This analysis shall be integrated with the software maintenance process (§6.10) and software problem resolution process (§6.13).

**Expected Outcome**: Closes three critical compliance gaps; provides explicit procedure for change risk management

---

#### 2. Add SOUP Anomaly Evaluation Requirement (§7.1.3)

**Target**: Addresses gap #1

**Action**: Add to §6.11 after paragraph 1:

> **SOUP Anomaly Evaluation**: For SOUP items identified as potentially contributing to hazardous situations, the manufacturer shall evaluate published anomaly lists for the specific version used, including:
>
> - Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVE) databases
> - Vendor security bulletins and release notes
> - Known defect lists and patch histories
>
> The evaluation shall determine if any known anomalies could result in a sequence of events leading to a hazardous situation. Evaluations shall be documented in the Risk Management File and repeated when:
>
> - SOUP versions are updated
> - New anomalies are published for SOUP items in use
> - During periodic post-market surveillance reviews
>
> For cybersecurity vulnerabilities in SOUP, see D0029257, Global Work Instruction, Cybersecurity for Medical Devices.

**Expected Outcome**: Establishes explicit SOUP anomaly evaluation process; integrates with cybersecurity work instruction

---

#### 3. Add Risk Control Verification and "New Hazard" Review (§7.3.1)

**Target**: Addresses gap #2

**Action**: Add to §6.11 after paragraph 3 (software safety class assignment):

> **Verification of Risk Control Measures**: The implementation of each risk control measure shall be verified, and the verification shall be documented in both:
>
> - Risk Management File
> - Software Verification documentation (D0017982, Software Verification Protocol)
>
> **New Hazard Review**: Each risk control measure shall be reviewed to determine if its implementation could result in a new hazardous situation. If new hazards are identified, they shall be addressed through the risk management process per D0003103.
>
> Risk control measure verification shall be explicitly included in Software Verification Protocols and traced per §7.3.3 (Traceability).

**Expected Outcome**: Establishes explicit verification requirement; adds critical "new hazard" review; integrates with verification process (§6.8)

---

### High-Priority Actions

#### 4. Add Systematic Hazard Analysis Procedures (§7.1.1, §7.1.2, §7.1.4)

**Target**: Addresses gaps #6, #7, #8

**Action**: Add detailed subsection to §6.11:

> **6.11.x Software Hazard Analysis**
>
> **Identify Software Items Contributing to Hazards** (Class B, C):
>
> 1. Review system-level hazardous situations identified in the Risk Management File (per D0003103 and ISO 14971)
> 2. For each hazardous situation, identify software items (including SOUP) that could contribute to the hazard
> 3. Document the linkage between hazardous situations and software items
>
> **Identify Potential Causes** (Class B, C):
> For each software item identified above, analyze potential causes of contribution to hazardous situations, considering:
>
> - a) Incorrect or incomplete specification of functionality
> - b) Software defects in the identified software item functionality
> - c) Failure or unexpected results from SOUP
> - d) Hardware failures or other software defects resulting in unpredictable software operation
> - e) Reasonably foreseeable misuse
>
> Techniques for cause analysis may include: software FMEA, fault tree analysis, use case/misuse case analysis, and threat modeling (for cybersecurity hazards per D0029257).
>
> **Document Potential Causes**:
> All potential causes identified shall be documented in the Risk Management File, including:
>
> - Software item identification
> - Associated hazardous situation(s)
> - Potential causes by category (a-e above)
> - Analysis method used
> - Resulting risk level determination

**Expected Outcome**: Provides explicit, step-by-step procedures for hazard analysis; addresses the five IEC 62304 cause categories; establishes documentation requirements

---

#### 5. Enhance Traceability Documentation Guidance (§7.3.3)

**Target**: Addresses procedural gap in otherwise compliant requirement

**Action**: Enhance §6.11 paragraph 5:

> **Traceability of Software Hazards** (Class B, C):
> Traceability of software hazards shall be documented as appropriate from the hazardous situation to the software item to the specific software cause to the risk control measure to the verification of the risk control measure.
>
> **Documentation Method**: Traceability shall be documented using D0017985, Global Template, Software Traceability Matrix, or an equivalent requirements management tool. Traceability shall be bidirectional, enabling both forward tracing (hazard → verification) and backward tracing (verification → hazard).
>
> **Integration**: Risk traceability shall be integrated with requirements traceability (§6.3), design traceability (§6.4, §6.5), and test traceability (§6.8) to provide complete end-to-end traceability.
>
> **Extent**: The extent of traceability documentation shall be commensurate with the software safety class and risk level. Higher risks require more detailed traceability.

**Expected Outcome**: Transforms textual requirement into actionable procedure; clarifies documentation methods; establishes integration with other traceability requirements

### Medium-Priority Enhancements

#### 6. Add Risk Control Definition Procedure (§7.2.1)

**Action**: Add to §6.11 after SOUP anomaly evaluation section:

> **Define Risk Control Measures** (Class B, C):
> For each software item that could contribute to a hazardous situation, risk control measures shall be defined and documented in the Risk Management File in accordance with ISO 14971 and D0003103.
>
> Risk control measures shall follow the ISO 14971 hierarchy:
>
> 1. Inherently safe design (eliminate hazard)
> 2. Protective measures (mitigate risk)
> 3. Information for safety (inform users)
>
> Software-specific risk control strategies may include:
>
> - Input validation and sanitization
> - Fail-safe states and error handling
> - Redundancy and diversity
> - Defensive programming techniques
> - User interface design for error prevention
> - Alarms and warnings
> - Cybersecurity controls (per D0029257)
>
> The adequacy of risk control measures shall be evaluated per ISO 14971 residual risk acceptance criteria.

**Expected Outcome**: Provides explicit procedure for defining risk controls; establishes ISO 14971 hierarchy; offers software-specific examples
