**Scope**: Clause 9 - Software Problem Resolution Process

## Executive Summary

D0003329 Rev 03 §6.13 provides **minimal coverage** of IEC 62304 Clause 9 (Software Problem Resolution Process) requirements. The work instruction addresses problem resolution at a **high conceptual level** but lacks the **specific procedural detail** and **systematic structure** required for full IEC 62304 compliance. While the document references the problem resolution process in multiple sections (§6.2, §6.7, §6.8, §6.10), the actual implementation requirements in §6.13 are significantly underdeveloped.

**Key Compliance Concerns**:

- **Critical gaps** in problem investigation procedures (§9.2)
- **Missing requirements** for problem report content/format (§9.1)
- **No explicit procedures** for notifying relevant parties (§9.3)
- **Insufficient guidance** on trend analysis methodology (§9.6)
- **Minimal detail** on verification of problem resolution (§9.7)
- **Missing integration** with D0003325 (Nonconformance/Complaint Management)
- **No test documentation** requirements for problem resolution testing (§9.8)

**Overall Compliance**: ~35% (3 of 8 subsections adequately addressed)

## Section 9.1: Prepare Problem Reports

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "The MANUFACTURER shall prepare a PROBLEM REPORT for each problem detected in the MEDICAL DEVICE SOFTWARE. PROBLEM REPORTS shall include a statement of criticality (for example, effect on performance, SAFETY, or SECURITY) as well as other information that may aid in the resolution of the problem (for example, devices affected, supported accessories affected). [Class A, B, C]"

### Compliant Elements ✅

**None** - D0003329 §6.13 does not explicitly address problem report preparation requirements.

### Partial Compliance ⚠️

**1. Implicit Problem Reporting**
**D0003329 Reference**: §6.13 states "All anomalies identified shall be investigated..."
**Analysis**: The work instruction acknowledges that anomalies must be identified but does not specify:

- What constitutes a problem report
- Required content/format for problem reports
- Statement of criticality requirements (effect on performance, safety, security)
- Information to aid resolution (devices affected, accessories affected)

**2. Template Reference**
**D0003329 Reference**: §6.7, §6.8 mention "D0004198 Global Template, Software Anomaly Report, or equivalent"
**Analysis**: While a template is referenced for anomalies during testing, there is no:

- Requirement to use this template for all problems (including post-release)
- Specification of mandatory fields in the template
- Mapping to IEC 62304 §9.1 requirements
- Explicit statement that this template serves as the PROBLEM REPORT mechanism

**3. Defect Tracking Tools**
**D0003329 Reference**: §6.7, §6.8 state "Software anomalies may also be tracked from creation to closure using a defect tracking software (e.g., Jira, Azure DevOps)"
**Analysis**: Tool usage is mentioned but there is no:

- Requirement for what fields/data must be captured
- Standard for criticality classification
- Guidance on problem report content regardless of tool

### Non-Compliant/Missing ❌

| **Problem Context Information**           | Missing | No requirement to capture devices affected, accessories affected, or other resolution-aiding information     |
| **Problem Report Format/Content**         | Missing | No specification of required problem report fields or content                                                |

## Section 9.2: Investigate the Problem

**IEC 62304 Requirement**:

> "The MANUFACTURER shall:
> a) investigate the problem and if possible identify the causes;
> b) EVALUATE the problem's relevance to SAFETY using the software RISK MANAGEMENT PROCESS (Clause 7);
> c) document the outcome of the investigation and evaluation; and
> d) create a CHANGE REQUEST(S) for actions needed to correct the problem, or document the rationale for taking no action. [Class A, B, C]"

### Compliant Elements ✅

**1. Investigation and Safety Evaluation**
**D0003329 Reference**: §6.13 states "All anomalies identified shall be investigated, evaluated for safety..."
**Analysis**: This statement demonstrates **awareness** of the investigation and safety evaluation requirements, but lacks procedural depth.

**2. Change Request or Rationale**
**D0003329 Reference**: §6.13 states "...and will result in a change request or rationale for taking no action."
**Analysis**: This directly addresses IEC 62304 §9.2(d) by acknowledging two acceptable outcomes of problem investigation.

### Partial Compliance ⚠️

**1. Investigation Procedures**
**Gap**: D0003329 does not specify:

- **How** problems are investigated (methodology, root cause analysis techniques)
- **Who** is responsible for investigation
- **When** investigation must occur (timeframes based on criticality)
- **Depth** of investigation required based on software safety class or problem severity

**2. Risk Management Integration**
**D0003329 Reference**: §6.11 requires risk management per D0003103, and §6.13 mentions "evaluated for safety"
**Gap**: Missing explicit requirement to use the software risk management process (Clause 7) for evaluating problem relevance to safety. The connection between problem evaluation and the risk management file is implied but not procedurally defined.

**3. Documentation Requirements**
**Gap**: §6.13 does not specify:

- **What** must be documented in investigation outcomes
- **Where** outcomes are documented (DHF, risk file, problem report?)
- **Format/Content** of investigation documentation
- **Approval/Review** requirements for investigation outcomes

### Non-Compliant/Missing ❌

| Requirement                       | Status  | Gap Description                                                                              |
| --------------------------------- | ------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Investigation Methodology**     | Missing | No procedure for how problems are investigated or causes identified                          |
| **Root Cause Analysis**           | Missing | No requirement for root cause analysis techniques or depth                                   |
| **Safety Evaluation Procedure**   | Partial | Safety evaluation mentioned but not explicitly tied to software risk management process (§7) |
| **Investigation Timeframes**      | Missing | No requirements for when investigation must be completed based on problem criticality        |
| **Investigation Responsibility**  | Missing | No assignment of roles/responsibilities for problem investigation                            |
| **Documentation Format/Location** | Missing | No specification of where investigation outcomes are documented                              |
| **Investigation Approval**        | Missing | No requirement for review/approval of investigation findings                                 |

**Risk Level**: **CRITICAL** - Without clear investigation procedures, problems may not be properly analyzed, safety relevance may be misjudged, and root causes may not be identified, leading to inadequate or incorrect corrective actions.

- Change requests arising from problem resolution **must** be approved

- §6.13 mentioning change requests

- §6.10 (Maintenance): Problem resolution process used for post-release problems

**Overall Assessment**: Anomaly tracking is addressed at key lifecycle phases, but the **continuity** of problem tracking across phases is not explicitly established. A single anomaly may be discovered during development, deferred as "known residual," and later addressed during maintenance. D0003329 does not clearly require that such anomalies maintain a continuous problem report record across these transitions.

## Recommendations for Remediation

### Priority 1: Critical Gaps (Immediate Action Required)

**1. Expand §6.13 to Include §9.1 Problem Report Requirements**

**Action**: Revise §6.13 to explicitly require:

```
Problem Report Preparation (IEC 62304 §9.1):
- A problem report shall be prepared for each problem detected in the medical device software at any lifecycle phase (development, testing, release, maintenance)
- Problem reports shall use D0004198, Global Template, Software Anomaly Report, or equivalent defect tracking tool (e.g., Jira, Azure DevOps)
- Each problem report shall include:
  a) Statement of criticality: classification of effect on performance, safety, or security (e.g., Critical/Major/Minor)
  b) Devices affected: product models, versions, or configurations impacted
  c) Accessories affected: related components or systems affected
  d) Information to aid resolution: reproduction steps, logs, screenshots, environment details
  e) Discovery phase: when/where problem was found (unit test, integration test, customer complaint, etc.)
```

**2. Expand §6.13 to Include §9.2 Investigation Procedures**

**Action**: Add investigation requirements:

```
Problem Investigation (IEC 62304 §9.2):
- All problems shall be investigated to identify root causes where possible
- Investigation shall include:
  a) Root cause analysis using appropriate techniques (e.g., 5-Why, fishbone diagram)
  b) Evaluation of problem relevance to safety using the software risk management process (§6.11, IEC 62304 §7)
  c) Documentation of investigation outcomes in the problem report or linked investigation record
  d) Creation of change request(s) for corrective actions, or documentation of rationale for taking no action
- Investigation timeframes shall be based on problem criticality:
  a) Critical (safety-relevant): Investigation initiated within 24 hours, completed within 5 business days
  b) Major: Investigation completed within 10 business days
  c) Minor: Investigation completed within 20 business days
- Investigation responsibility: [Define role - e.g., Software Lead, assigned by Project Manager]
```

**3. Expand §6.13 to Include §9.5 Record-Keeping Requirements**

**Action**: Add record-keeping requirements:

```
Problem Resolution Records (IEC 62304 §9.5):
- Records of all problem reports and their resolution (including verification) shall be maintained in the project's defect tracking system and/or Design History File
- Records shall be retained for at least the life of the software product or as long as the product is supported for human use, whichever comes first
- The risk management file shall be updated when safety-relevant problems are identified or resolved
- Records shall be retrievable and auditable for regulatory inspection
```

**4. Add §9.6 Trend Analysis Requirement to §6.13**

**Action**: Add trend analysis requirement:

```
Problem Trend Analysis (IEC 62304 §9.6):
- Problem reports shall be analyzed periodically to detect trends that may indicate systemic issues
- Trend analysis shall be performed at minimum:
  a) At each major milestone (design review, release candidate, production release)
  b) Quarterly for products in maintenance
  c) When triggered by adverse events or complaint patterns
- Trend analysis shall evaluate:
  a) Frequency of problems by category, root cause, or affected component
  b) Severity trends (increasing critical problems)
  c) Recurrence of similar problems across releases or products
- Trend analysis results shall be documented and reviewed by [Quality/Management]
- Adverse trends shall trigger corrective action via the CAPA process
- Integration: Trend analysis shall coordinate with D0003325 (Nonconformance) complaint trending and D0003293 (Post-Market Surveillance, if applicable)
```

### Priority 2: Significant Gaps (Address in Next Revision)

**5. Add §9.3 Notification Procedures to §6.13**

**Action**: Define notification requirements:

```
Notification of Relevant Parties (IEC 62304 §9.3):
- Relevant parties shall be advised of problems as appropriate based on problem criticality and impact
- Internal notification:
  a) Critical/safety-relevant problems: Notify Quality, Management, Regulatory, affected project teams within 24 hours
  b) Cross-product problems: Notify all affected product development teams
- External notification:
  a) Post-release problems: Follow Software Maintenance Plan (§6.10) and regulatory requirements
  b) Regulatory reporting: Coordinate with Regulatory Affairs per D0003325 and applicable regulations
  c) Customer notification: As required by D0003325 for complaints or safety issues
- Notification content shall include: problem description, affected products, safety implications, proposed actions, and timelines
```

**6. Strengthen §9.7 Verification Requirements in §6.13**

**Action**: Clarify verification requirements:

```
Verification of Problem Resolution (IEC 62304 §9.7):
- All problem resolutions shall be verified to confirm:
  a) The problem has been resolved and the problem report can be closed
  b) Adverse trends (if identified) have been reversed
  c) Change request(s) have been implemented in the appropriate software and documentation
  d) No additional problems have been introduced (verified by regression testing)
- Verification shall include:
  a) Re-execution of original test case that detected the problem (if applicable)
  b) Regression testing per Software Verification Protocol (D0017982) to confirm no side effects
  c) Code review of changes (for Class B/C)
  d) Updated test documentation per IEC 62304 §9.8 requirements (see below)
- Verification may be performed by original tester or independent verifier as defined in Software Development Plan
- Verification results shall be documented in the problem report and Software Verification Report (D0017983)
```

**7. Explicitly Map §9.8 Test Documentation to §6.13**

**Action**: Add test documentation requirements to §6.13:

```
Test Documentation for Problem Resolution (IEC 62304 §9.8):
- When testing, retesting, or regression testing following a problem resolution change, test documentation shall include:
  a) Test results (pass/fail)
  b) Anomalies found during verification (if any)
  c) Version of software tested
  d) Relevant hardware and software test configurations
  e) Relevant test tools used
  f) Date tested
  g) Identification of the tester
- This documentation shall be captured in Software Verification Report (D0017983) or equivalent test execution record
- For problem resolution during maintenance, test documentation shall be retained in the maintenance records
```

### Priority 3: Integration and Clarity (Continuous Improvement)

**8. Establish Clear Interface with D0003325 (Nonconformance)**

**Action**: Add procedural linkage in §6.13:

```
Integration with Complaint Handling (D0003325):
- Software-related complaints received through D0003325, Global Procedure, Nonconformance shall trigger the software problem resolution process
- Criteria for escalation to D0003325:
  a) Problems discovered post-market that may require customer notification or regulatory reporting
  b) Problems with potential safety implications
  c) Problems that may constitute a nonconformance to product specifications
- The problem resolution process (IEC 62304 Clause 9) and complaint handling process (D0003325) shall coordinate to ensure:
  a) Single source of truth for problem tracking (cross-reference between systems)
  b) Consistent investigation and root cause analysis
  c) Coordinated CAPA implementation
  d) Unified trending and reporting
- [Define responsibility] shall serve as liaison between software development and complaint management
```

## Conclusion

D0003329 Rev 03 §6.13 demonstrates **awareness** of the software problem resolution process but lacks the **procedural depth and specificity** required for full IEC 62304 Clause 9 compliance. The work instruction addresses problem resolution at a **high conceptual level** (~5 sentences), whereas IEC 62304 Clause 9 is a detailed process with 8 distinct requirements spanning problem identification, investigation, resolution, verification, and trending.

**Key Compliance Issues**:

1. **§6.13 is underdeveloped**: The problem resolution section needs significant expansion to address §9.1-§9.8 requirements explicitly
2. **Cross-references exist but are insufficient**: While §6.2, §6.7, §6.8, and §6.10 reference problem resolution, these do not substitute for comprehensive requirements in §6.13
3. **Integration with other procedures is incomplete**: Particularly the interface with D0003325 (Nonconformance/Complaint Handling) and trend analysis coordination with post-market surveillance

**Recommended Approach**:

- **Immediate**: Expand §6.13 to ~2-3 pages covering all §9.1-§9.8 requirements explicitly
- **Short-term**: Verify supporting templates (D0004198) and procedures (D0003325) align with Clause 9 requirements
- **Long-term**: Establish integrated problem resolution, complaint management, and CAPA system with unified trending and reporting

With comprehensive remediation, D0003329 can achieve full Clause 9 compliance and establish a robust problem resolution process that supports both regulatory compliance and continuous quality improvement.
