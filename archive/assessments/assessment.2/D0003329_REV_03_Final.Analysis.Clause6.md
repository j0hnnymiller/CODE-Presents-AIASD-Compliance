# IEC 62304 Clause 6 (Software Maintenance) Compliance Assessment

## D0003329 Rev 03 - Software Maintenance Process

**Assessment Date**: December 16, 2025
**Assessor**: Systematic Compliance Analysis
**Standard**: BS EN 62304:2006+A1:2015 (IEC 62304)
**Scope**: Clause 6 - Software Maintenance Process

---

## Executive Summary

D0003329 Rev 03 §6.10 "Software Maintenance" provides **partial compliance** with IEC 62304 Clause 6 requirements. The work instruction addresses most fundamental maintenance planning requirements (§6.1) but has **critical gaps** in the problem and modification analysis process (§6.2 and §6.3). Specifically:

**Strengths:**

- ✅ Establishes requirement for Software Maintenance Plan (§6.1)
- ✅ Addresses feedback procedures, problem criteria, and process integration (§6.1.a-e)
- ✅ References SOUP management procedures (§6.1.f)
- ✅ Mentions user/regulator notification (§6.1.g)
- ✅ Integration with D0003325 (Nonconformance) for complaint handling

**Critical Deficiencies:**

- ❌ **Missing §6.2.1**: No explicit requirement to monitor, document, and evaluate feedback
- ❌ **Missing §6.2.2**: Problem resolution process reference incomplete
- ❌ **Missing §6.2.3**: No requirement to analyze problems for trends
- ❌ **Missing §6.2.4**: No change request approval process
- ❌ **Missing §6.2.5**: No user/regulator communication requirements for changes
- ❌ **Missing §6.3**: Complete absence of modification implementation requirements
- ❌ **Missing integration**: No explicit linkage to D0003098 design change process (§6.10)

**Regulatory Risk**: **HIGH** - Missing structured problem analysis and modification implementation processes creates compliance gaps that could impact audit findings and post-market surveillance effectiveness.

### 6.1.g: User and Regulator Notification

**IEC 62304 Requirement:**

> "As required by local regulation, the MANUFACTURER shall inform users and regulators about:
> a) any problem in released MEDICAL DEVICE SOFTWARE and the consequences of continued unchanged use; and
> b) the nature of any available changes to released MEDICAL DEVICE SOFTWARE and how to obtain and install the changes." [Class A, B, C]

**Compliant Elements** ✅

| IEC § | D0003329 §           | Requirement Coverage    | Evidence                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| ----- | -------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 6.1.g | §6.10 (line 310-311) | Notification procedures | "As required by local regulation, the manufacturer may need to inform users and regulators regarding any problem with the released software and consequence of continued use along with the nature of any available software, how to obtain, and install these changes." |

**Partial Compliance** ⚠️

**Analysis:**
D0003329 addresses user/regulator notification but with critical weaknesses:

**Deficiencies:**

1. **"may need to"** - IEC 62304 uses "shall" (mandatory). The word "may" suggests optionality, which contradicts the standard
2. **Incomplete coverage** - IEC 62304 §6.2.5 requires:

   - Identification of approved change requests affecting released software
   - Information about problems AND consequences of continued use
   - Nature of changes AND how to obtain/install

   D0003329 combines these into a single statement but lacks the structured approach required by §6.2.5

3. **Missing operational details:**
   - No reference to who performs notification (Regulatory Affairs?)
   - No integration with post-market surveillance (D0003293) or Medical Device Reporting
   - No linkage to Field Safety Corrective Actions (FSCA) or Field Safety Notices (FSN)

**Regulatory Risk:** **SIGNIFICANT** - Weak language ("may") for a mandatory requirement, and missing integration with regulatory reporting obligations.

**Recommendation:**

- Change "may need to" to "shall"
- Add reference to D0003293 (Post-Market Surveillance)
- Add reference to applicable regulatory reporting procedures (MDR, FSCA, FSN)
- Explicitly address §6.2.5 requirements in problem analysis section

## Section 6.2: Problem and Modification Analysis

### 6.2.1: Document and Evaluate Feedback

**IEC 62304 Requirements:**

**§6.2.1.1 Monitor feedback:**

> "The MANUFACTURER shall monitor feedback on MEDICAL DEVICE SOFTWARE released for intended use." [Class A, B, C]

**§6.2.1.2 Document and EVALUATE feedback:**

> "Feedback shall be documented and EVALUATED to determine whether a problem exists in a released MEDICAL DEVICE SOFTWARE. Any such problem shall be recorded as a PROBLEM REPORT (see Clause 9). PROBLEM REPORTS shall include actual or potential adverse events, and deviations from specifications." [Class A, B, C]

**§6.2.1.3 Evaluate PROBLEM REPORT's affects on SAFETY:**

> "Each PROBLEM REPORT shall be EVALUATED to determine how it affects the SAFETY of MEDICAL DEVICE SOFTWARE released for intended use (see 9.2) and whether a change to that software is needed to address the problem." [Class A, B, C]

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

**D0003329 Coverage:**
D0003329 §6.10 addresses feedback procedures in the maintenance plan (line 303-304) but **DOES NOT** explicitly require:

1. Active monitoring of feedback after release
2. Documentation and evaluation of feedback to determine if it's a problem
3. Recording problems as Problem Reports (Clause 9 integration)
4. Inclusion of "actual or potential adverse events"
5. Safety evaluation of each problem report

**Gap Analysis:**

| IEC §   | Requirement                | D0003329 §      | Status | Gap Description                                                |
| ------- | -------------------------- | --------------- | ------ | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 6.2.1.1 | Monitor feedback           | Missing         | ❌     | No explicit requirement to monitor released software           |
| 6.2.1.2 | Document/evaluate feedback | §6.10 (partial) | ⚠️     | Procedures required in plan but not operational requirement    |
| 6.2.1.2 | Record as Problem Report   | §6.13 (partial) | ⚠️     | §6.13 addresses anomalies but not post-release problem reports |
| 6.2.1.2 | Include adverse events     | Missing         | ❌     | No mention of adverse event inclusion                          |
| 6.2.1.3 | Safety evaluation          | Missing         | ❌     | No requirement to evaluate safety impact of problems           |

**Evidence of Partial Coverage:**

- Line 335: "Once the product is developed and in production, anomalies shall be handled in accordance with the Software Maintenance Plan."
- Line 335-336: "Any complaints for which an action is needed by the software team shall be logged and tracked until it is dispositioned."
- Reference to D0003325 (Nonconformance) provides complaint escalation process

**Critical Deficiencies:**

1. **No Active Monitoring Requirement:** §6.2.1.1 requires manufacturers to **actively monitor** feedback. D0003329 §6.10 requires procedures in the plan but doesn't mandate operational monitoring.

2. **Reactive vs. Proactive:** The work instruction is complaint-driven ("complaints for which an action is needed") rather than feedback-monitoring driven as required by IEC 62304.

3. **Missing Safety Evaluation:** §6.2.1.3 requires explicit safety evaluation of each problem report. D0003329 §6.11 addresses software risk management but doesn't connect it to post-release problem evaluation.

4. **Adverse Event Gap:** IEC 62304 §6.2.1.2 explicitly requires problem reports to include "actual or potential adverse events." No mention in D0003329.

5. **Problem Report Formalization:** §6.13 (Problem Resolution) addresses anomalies generally but doesn't specifically address the post-release Problem Report requirements of §6.2.1.2.

**Regulatory Risk:** **CRITICAL** - Missing structured post-market monitoring and safety evaluation process for software creates significant compliance gap and potential patient safety risk.

**Integration Gaps:**

- D0003098 §6.8 (Phase 7 Post Release) exists but no explicit connection to D0003329 §6.10
- D0003293 (Post-Market Surveillance) not referenced in §6.10
- D0003325 (Nonconformance) mentioned but integration incomplete

## Section 6.3: Modification Implementation

### 6.3.1: Use Established Process to Implement Modification

**IEC 62304 Requirement:**

> "The MANUFACTURER shall identify and perform any Clause 5 ACTIVITIES that need to be repeated as a result of the modification." [Class A, B, C]

**Non-Compliant/Missing** ❌

**D0003329 Coverage:** No content in §6.10 addressing modification implementation.

**Gap:**
IEC 62304 §6.3.1 requires manufacturers to identify which development activities from Clause 5 must be repeated when implementing maintenance modifications. This is a **critical requirement** ensuring that software changes undergo appropriate development lifecycle activities.

D0003329 §6.10 is **silent** on modification implementation. It addresses planning (§6.1 requirements) but not execution.

**Potential Coverage in Other Sections:**

- D0003098 §6.10 (Design Changes) addresses change implementation but doesn't explicitly reference IEC 62304 §6.3.1
- D0003336 (Change Impact Assessment) may address which activities to repeat, but not referenced in D0003329

**Missing Elements:**

1. Requirement to identify applicable Clause 5 activities for each modification
2. Criteria for determining which activities to repeat (risk-based?)
3. Documentation requirements for modification implementation
4. Integration between maintenance (§6.10) and development (§6.2-6.9)

**Regulatory Risk:** **CRITICAL** - Missing guidance on how to implement software modifications ensures that changes bypass required development activities, creating quality and safety risks.

**Recommendation:**
Add new section to D0003329 §6.10:

```
6.10.X Software Maintenance Modification Implementation

Modifications to released software shall be implemented by repeating applicable Clause 5 activities per IEC 62304 §6.3. The Software Maintenance Plan shall identify which development activities (§6.2 through §6.9) shall be repeated based on:
- Scope and complexity of the modification
- Software safety classification
- Risk analysis per §6.11
- Change impact assessment per D0003336

At minimum, modifications shall include:
- Requirements analysis (§6.3) for the change
- Impact assessment on architecture and detailed design (§6.4, §6.5)
- Implementation and unit testing (§6.6)
- Integration testing for modified components (§6.7)
- Regression testing to detect unintended effects (§6.8)
- Risk management for the change (§6.11)
- Configuration management per §6.12
- Release documentation per §6.9
```

## Remediation Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (Critical Gaps)

**1. Add Section 6.10.1: Post-Release Problem Analysis**

Insert after line 311 (end of current §6.10 content):

```markdown
6.10.1 Post-Release Problem Analysis and Modification

Once software is released to production, the following activities shall be performed:

a) Feedback Monitoring: The manufacturer shall actively monitor feedback on released software per IEC 62304 §6.2.1.1. Feedback sources shall include but not be limited to:

- Customer complaints per D0003325 (Nonconformance)
- Post-market surveillance data per D0003293
- Technical support inquiries
- Internal testing and verification results
- Published SOUP anomaly lists

b) Problem Documentation and Evaluation: Feedback shall be documented and evaluated to determine whether a problem exists per IEC 62304 §6.2.1.2. Problems shall be recorded as Problem Reports including:

- Actual or potential adverse events
- Deviations from specifications
- Safety-related anomalies
- Unexpected software behavior

c) Safety Evaluation: Each Problem Report shall be evaluated for its effect on safety per IEC 62304 §6.2.1.3 and §6.11 (Risk Management). The evaluation shall determine:

- Whether the problem affects safety of released software
- Whether a software change is needed to address the problem
- Whether the current software safety classification remains appropriate

d) Problem Resolution: Problem Reports shall be managed per §6.13 (Software Problem Resolution Process) and IEC 62304 Clause 9.

e) Change Request Analysis: Change requests for released software shall be analyzed per IEC 62304 §6.2.3 for effects on:

- The organization (resources, processes, documentation)
- Released medical device software (functionality, performance, safety)
- Systems with which the software interfaces (interoperability, compatibility)

Change impact assessment shall be performed per D0003336 (Change Impact Assessment).

f) Change Request Approval: Change requests modifying released software shall be evaluated and approved per IEC 62304 §6.2.4 before implementation. Approval shall be managed through D0003101 (Global Procedure, Change Management) and documented per §6.12 (Configuration Management).

g) User and Regulator Communication: Approved change requests affecting released software shall be identified per IEC 62304 §6.2.5. As required by local regulation, the manufacturer shall inform users and regulators about:
i) Any problem in released software and the consequences of continued unchanged use
ii) The nature of available software changes and how to obtain and install the changes

Communication shall integrate with D0003293 (Post-Market Surveillance) and applicable regulatory reporting requirements (MDR, FSCA, FSN, per local regulations).
```

**2. Add Section 6.10.2: Software Modification Implementation**

```markdown
6.10.2 Software Modification Implementation

Modifications to released software shall be implemented per IEC 62304 §6.3 by repeating applicable Clause 5 activities. The Software Maintenance Plan shall identify which development activities (§6.2 through §6.9) shall be repeated based on:

- Scope and complexity of the modification
- Software safety classification
- Risk analysis per §6.11 and IEC 62304 §7.4
- Change impact assessment per D0003336

At minimum, modifications shall include:
a) Requirements analysis (§6.3) for the change
b) Impact assessment on architecture and detailed design (§6.4, §6.5)
c) Implementation and unit testing (§6.6)
d) Integration testing for modified components (§6.7)
e) Regression testing to detect unintended effects per IEC 62304 §5.7 and §6.8
f) Risk management for the change per §6.11 and IEC 62304 §7.4
g) Configuration management per §6.12
h) Release documentation per §6.9 and IEC 62304 §6.3.2

Modifications shall be released per §6.9 (Software Release) and IEC 62304 §5.8. Modifications may be released as:

- Full re-release of the software system with updated version, or
- Modification kit containing changed software items and installation tools

Release documentation shall include:

- Approval of the modification per D0003101 (Change Management)
- Known residual anomalies from the modification
- Configuration items affected by the modification
- User notification materials per §6.10 maintenance plan requirements
```
