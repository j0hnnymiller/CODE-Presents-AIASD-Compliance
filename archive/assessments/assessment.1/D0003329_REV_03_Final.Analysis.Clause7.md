# IEC 62304 Clause 7 (Software Risk Management Process) Compliance Assessment

## D0003329 Rev 03 - Software Risk Management Process

**Assessment Date**: December 16, 2025
**Assessor**: Medical-Device-Design-Controls-Expert (Systematic Compliance Analysis)

## Executive Summary

D0003329 Rev 03 Section 6.11 provides **moderate compliance** with IEC 62304 Clause 7 requirements for software risk management. The work instruction correctly addresses high-level integration with overall risk management (ISO 14971), risk control measure implementation, and software safety classification. However, **significant gaps exist in explicit procedural requirements** for hazard analysis sub-activities (§7.1), risk control measure definition (§7.2), verification procedures (§7.3), and change risk management (§7.4).

**Overall Compliance Status**:

- **Compliant Requirements**: 3 of 12 (25%)
- **Partial Compliance**: 7 of 12 (58%)
- **Non-Compliant/Missing**: 2 of 12 (17%)

**Key Strength**: D0003329 §6.11 correctly integrates software risk management with overall device risk management per ISO 14971 and D0003103, and addresses cybersecurity risk management integration (D0029257).

**Critical Finding**: D0003329 §6.11 lacks explicit step-by-step procedures for executing IEC 62304 Clause 7 requirements, particularly for hazard analysis methodology, SOUP anomaly evaluation, risk control verification, traceability documentation, and change risk assessment. The section reads more as high-level policy than operational procedure.

## Section 7.1: Analysis of Software Contributing to Hazardous Situations

**IEC 62304 §7.1 Context**:

> Software hazard analysis identifies software items that could contribute to hazardous situations, potential causes of contribution, and ensures SOUP anomalies are evaluated.

### 7.1.1: Identify Software Items That Could Contribute to Hazardous Situations

**IEC 62304 §7.1.1 Requirement**:

> "The MANUFACTURER shall identify SOFTWARE ITEMS that could contribute to a hazardous situation identified in the MEDICAL DEVICE RISK ANALYSIS ACTIVITY of ISO 14971 (see 4.2). [Class B, C]
> NOTE: The hazardous situation could be the direct result of software failure or the result of the failure of a RISK CONTROL measure that is implemented in software."

#### Compliant Elements ✅

| Element                                           | D0003329 Reference | Evidence                                                                                                             |

| Integration with overall risk management          | §6.11, Para 1      | "Risk analysis of products shall be in accordance with the overall project Risk Management Plan"                     |
| SOUP inclusion                                    | §6.11, Para 1      | Explicitly mentions "including SOUP"                                                                                 |

#### Partial Compliance ⚠️

| Element                                     | Gap Description                                                                       | Impact                                                                            |

## Cross-Reference Analysis

### Integration with ISO 14971 (Overall Risk Management)

**Assessment**: **Good Integration with Gaps**

#### Strengths ✅

- §6.11 Para 1: Explicitly states "Risk analysis of products shall be in accordance with the overall project Risk Management Plan"
- §6.11 Para 4: References D0003103 (Global Procedure, Risk Management) for risk management file
- Alignment with ISO 14971 risk management framework evident

#### Gaps ❌

- IEC 62304 Clause 7 provides _additional_ software-specific requirements beyond ISO 14971; D0003329 does not clearly delineate which requirements are ISO 14971 vs. IEC 62304-specific
- Missing explicit statement that software risk management is integrated with but supplementary to device risk management
- No guidance on when/how software hazard analysis feeds into overall device risk analysis

**Recommendation**: Add introductory statement in §6.11 clarifying that Clause 7 requirements are _in addition to_ ISO 14971 requirements implemented via D0003103, not replacements.

### Traceability Framework

**Assessment**: **Exemplary for §7.3.3, Insufficient for Overall Execution**

#### §7.3.3 Traceability (Compliant) ✅

- D0003329 §6.11 Para 5 provides complete, explicit traceability requirements
- All four required links documented (hazard → software item → cause → control → verification)
- Class B/C scoping correct

#### Execution Traceability (Gaps) ❌

- **Problem**: Excellent traceability _documentation_ requirement, but missing _execution_ procedures to populate the traceability
- No procedure for _how_ to:
  - Identify software items from hazardous situations (§7.1.1 execution)
  - Determine specific software causes (§7.1.2 execution)
  - Link causes to risk controls (§7.2 execution)
  - Verify risk controls (§7.3.1 execution)
- Traceability template reference missing (contrast with §6.8 which references D0017985 Software Traceability Matrix)

**Analogy**: D0003329 specifies the _output_ (complete traceability documentation) without specifying the _process_ to create the output.

**Recommendation**: Add procedure or template reference for executing and documenting software hazard traceability (similar to how D0017985 is referenced for requirements traceability).

## Remediation Recommendations

### Priority 1: Critical Gaps (Address Immediately)

#### Recommendation 1.1: Add §7.1 Hazard Analysis Procedures

**Action**: Expand D0003329 §6.11 to explicitly require all §7.1 sub-activities with procedural guidance.

**Proposed Addition to D0003329 §6.11** (new paragraphs after Para 1):

```
Software Hazard Analysis (Class B and C)

For Class B and C software, hazard analysis shall include the following activities:

a) Identify Software Items Contributing to Hazardous Situations (IEC 62304 §7.1.1)
   - Review medical device risk analysis per ISO 14971 and D0003103 to identify hazardous
     situations
   - Identify software items that could contribute to each hazardous situation, considering:
     * Direct contribution: Software failure directly causes hazardous situation
     * Indirect contribution: Failure of risk control measure implemented in software
   - Document identified software items in Risk Management File

b) Identify Potential Causes (IEC 62304 §7.1.2)
   For each software item identified in (a), identify potential causes of contribution to
   hazardous situations, considering:
   - Incorrect or incomplete specification of functionality
   - Software defects in the identified software item functionality
   - Failure or unexpected results from SOUP
   - Hardware failures or other software defects resulting in unpredictable software operation
   - Reasonably foreseeable misuse

c) Evaluate Published SOUP Anomaly Lists (IEC 62304 §7.1.3)
   If SOUP failure or unexpected results is a potential cause:
   - Obtain published anomaly lists from SOUP suppliers for the specific version used
   - Evaluate whether any known anomalies could result in a sequence of events leading to
     a hazardous situation
   - Document evaluation results in Risk Management File
   - Consider version upgrade if critical anomalies identified

d) Document Potential Causes (IEC 62304 §7.1.4)
   - Document all identified potential causes in the Risk Management File per D0003103
   - Maintain traceability per paragraph 5 below (§7.3.3)

Hazard analysis shall be performed during software architecture development and updated
throughout development lifecycle as software design evolves.
```

#### Recommendation 2.1: Add Hazard Analysis Methodology Guidance

**Action**: Provide practical guidance on _how_ to execute hazard analysis activities.

**Proposed Implementation**:

Option 1: Add Annex/Appendix to D0003329

```
Appendix X - Software Hazard Analysis Methodology

This appendix provides guidance for executing IEC 62304 Clause 7 software hazard analysis.

1. Identify Software Items Contributing to Hazards (§7.1.1)

   Step 1: Review Device Hazard Analysis
   - Obtain device hazard analysis from Risk Management File (D0003103)
   - Identify all hazardous situations involving device

   Step 2: Identify Software Contribution - Direct Failure
   - For each hazardous situation, ask: "Could software failure directly cause this hazard?"
   - If yes, identify which software item(s) could fail to cause hazard

   Step 3: Identify Software Contribution - Risk Control Failure
   - Review risk control measures for each hazardous situation
   - Identify which risk controls are implemented (wholly or partially) in software
   - If risk control fails, could hazardous situation occur?

   Step 4: Document Software Items
   - Create software hazard list linking: Hazardous Situation → Software Item → Type
     (Direct/Control)

2. Identify Potential Causes (§7.1.2)

   For each software item identified, perform systematic cause analysis using the five
   IEC 62304 categories:

   a) Incorrect/Incomplete Specification
      - Review software requirements (§6.3) for completeness, correctness, clarity
      - Identify requirements gaps or ambiguities that could lead to wrong implementation
      - Consider edge cases, boundary conditions not specified

   b) Software Defects
      - Consider potential coding errors (logic, calculation, data handling)
      - Consider integration issues between software items
      - Consider timing/synchronization issues

   c) SOUP Failure/Unexpected Results
      - Review SOUP items in architecture (§6.4)
      - Identify SOUP behaviors critical to safety
      - Consider SOUP anomalies, bugs, limitations
      - See §7.1.3 for anomaly list evaluation procedure

   d) Hardware/Software Interaction
      - Identify dependencies on hardware (sensors, actuators, processors)
      - Consider hardware failure modes (sensor drift, power loss, communication errors)
      - Consider how software responds to hardware faults

   e) Reasonably Foreseeable Misuse
      - Review intended use and known use errors (human factors analysis)
      - Consider incorrect user inputs, use in wrong clinical scenarios
      - Consider use by untrained personnel, use beyond intended lifespan

   Document all causes in Risk Management File with clear traceability to software item.

3. Evaluate SOUP Anomaly Lists (§7.1.3)

   For each SOUP item identified as potential cause in step 2(c):

   Step 1: Identify SOUP Details
   - SOUP name and supplier
   - Specific version/build number used in device
   - SOUP functionality relied upon

   Step 2: Obtain Anomaly Information
   - Check supplier website for known issues, CVE databases for security vulnerabilities
   - Review release notes, bug trackers, security advisories
   - Search developer forums for reported issues

   Step 3: Evaluate Relevance
   - For each known anomaly, determine if device uses affected SOUP functionality
   - Assess whether anomaly could lead to hazardous situation
   - Document evaluation: Anomaly ID → Device Impact → Hazard Contribution

   Step 4: Mitigation
   - If critical anomaly found: Consider SOUP version upgrade or alternative SOUP
   - If upgrade not feasible: Implement architectural mitigation (e.g., input validation,
     watchdog)
   - Document mitigation in Risk Management File

4. Documentation (§7.1.4)

   Risk Management File shall include:
   - Software hazard list (Hazardous Situation → Software Item → Causes)
   - SOUP anomaly evaluation results
   - Traceability per §7.3.3 (see paragraph 5 of §6.11)

5. Tools and Templates
   - Software Hazard Analysis Worksheet [to be developed]
   - SOUP Anomaly Evaluation Checklist [to be developed]
   - Software Risk Traceability Matrix (extend D0017985 or create separate)
```

## Regulatory Risk Assessment

### Impact of Non-Compliance

**High Risk (Critical Gaps)**:

- **Incomplete Hazard Analysis**: Missing §7.1.2 cause analysis and §7.1.3 SOUP anomaly evaluation could result in unidentified hazards, leading to patient harm
- **Unverified Risk Controls**: Missing §7.3.1 verification requirement means risk controls may not function as intended, creating false sense of safety
- **Unsafe Changes**: Missing §7.4 change risk analysis could result in changes introducing new hazards or disabling existing controls
- **Regulatory Submission Issues**: Regulatory reviewers will identify missing IEC 62304 §7 requirements, potentially leading to submission rejection or major deficiency findings
- **Audit Major Findings**: Missing critical risk management procedures will result in major audit findings requiring immediate corrective action

**Medium Risk (Significant Gaps)**:

- **Inconsistent Execution**: Lack of procedural guidance leads to inconsistent hazard analysis across projects, some adequate and some inadequate
- **Incomplete Documentation**: Partial compliance with documentation requirements may result in difficulty demonstrating risk management completeness during regulatory review
- **Process Maturity**: Relying on general ISO 14971 without IEC 62304-specific procedures suggests immature software risk management processes

**Low Risk (Minor Gaps)**:

- **Efficiency Loss**: Missing templates and tools reduces efficiency but doesn't directly impact safety
- **Training Gaps**: Lack of methodology guidance may require more training effort but is manageable

## Conclusion

D0003329 Rev 03 Section 6.11 provides a **foundation** for IEC 62304 Clause 7 compliance with particularly strong coverage of software-implemented risk controls (§7.2.2) and traceability documentation (§7.3.3). However, **significant procedural gaps** exist in hazard analysis execution (§7.1), verification requirements (§7.3.1), and change risk management (§7.4) that create regulatory compliance risk.

### Key Takeaways

1. **Policy vs. Procedure Gap**: §6.11 reads as high-level policy ("shall identify software items") rather than operational procedure ("how to identify software items")
2. **Critical §7.1 Gap**: Hazard analysis procedures (§7.1.2 cause analysis, §7.1.3 SOUP anomaly evaluation) are almost entirely missing, creating highest compliance risk
3. **Missing §7.4**: Software change risk management requirements not addressed in §6.11, though related processes exist in §6.10, §6.12
4. **Strong Foundations**: Excellent ISO 14971 integration, good §7.2.2 and §7.3.3 coverage, strong cybersecurity integration provide foundation to build upon
5. **Integration Opportunity**: Related processes (maintenance §6.10, configuration management §6.12, problem resolution §6.13) exist but need explicit §7.4 integration

### Recommended Next Steps

1. **Immediate Action** (Priority 1): Implement critical gap remediation (§7.1 procedures, §7.3.1 verification, §7.4 change risk management) - Weeks 1-4
2. **Short-Term** (Priority 2): Develop methodology guidance, clarify ISO 14971 integration, add SOUP change procedures - Weeks 5-8
3. **Continuous Improvement** (Priority 3): Implement minor enhancements, develop tools/templates, train personnel - Weeks 9-12
4. **Pilot Application**: Apply revised procedures to one project to validate practicality and identify refinements
5. **Update D0003103**: Ensure D0003103 (Global Procedure, Risk Management) provides adequate support for IEC 62304 Clause 7 documentation in Risk Management File

### Compliance Confidence Level

**Current State**: **Moderate Partial Compliance** - Strong foundations with critical procedural gaps
**Post-Remediation (Priority 1)**: **Good Compliance** - Critical requirements addressed with operational procedures
**Post-Remediation (All)**: **Exemplary** - Comprehensive risk management with methodology guidance enabling consistent, auditable execution
