PURPOSE
This work instruction describes the requirements and activities for development of software in support of Software in a Medical Device (SiMD) and Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) within Acme.

SCOPE
This work instruction is applicable to new medical device designs as well as modifications or improvements to existing device designs. This work instruction applies to all medical devices for which Acme is responsible for the design whether it is carried out by Acme, or on Acme's behalf by a third party.
For non-medical device software, this work instruction may be used to guide the activities performed.
This work instruction does not apply to non-device Software. For non-device software, refer to D0003078, Global Procedure, Non-Device Software Validation.

## 6.1 Legacy Software (BS EN 62304 section 4.4)

For medical device software that was developed for which there is insufficient objective evidence that it was developed according to the current BS EN 62304 standard, compliance may be demonstrated by either:

1. Applying BS EN 62304 Clauses 4.4.2 through 4.4.5, or
2. Applying BS EN 62304 Clauses 5 through 9

Applying BS EN 62304 Clauses 4.4.2 through 4.4.5 may be a less burdensome approach.

### 6.1.1 Software Safety Classification for Legacy Software

Prior to applying the legacy software compliance pathway (§4.4), the legacy software system shall be classified according to BS EN 62304 §4.3 Software Safety Classification (see Appendix 10.1 Software Safety Classification). The software safety classification (Class A, B, or C) determines the scope of required deliverables in the subsequent gap analysis (§4.4.3).

### 6.1.2 Decision Criteria for Legacy Software Pathway

The legacy software pathway (BS EN 62304 Clauses 4.4.2-4.4.5) shall be used when:

- The software was on market before the organization's adoption of IEC 62304 compliance requirements
- Insufficient objective evidence exists of original IEC 62304-compliant development processes
- Cost-benefit analysis demonstrates that the §4.4 pathway provides equivalent safety assurance compared to full §5-9 compliance
- Risk assessment confirms the legacy software approach is appropriate for the device's risk profile

### 6.1.3 Legacy Software Risk Management Activities (BS EN 62304 section 4.4.2)

To accomplish compliance through the legacy software method, the following shall be accomplished:

#### 6.1.3.1 Risk Management Activities

Risk management activities shall be performed to establish the risk associated with the continued use of legacy software, incorporating any available feedback including post-production information including incidents and near incidents. The risk management activities shall include all five elements specified in BS EN 62304 §4.4.2.b:

1. **Integration in Overall Medical Device Architecture**: Analyze how the legacy software integrates within the overall medical device architecture, including interfaces with other software systems, hardware components, and external systems.

2. **Continuing Validity of Risk Control Measures**: Verify that existing risk control measures implemented in or by the legacy software remain effective and appropriate for current intended use and operating environment.

3. **Identification of Hazardous Situations**: Identify any hazardous situations that could result from the continued use of the legacy software, including previously unrecognized hazards based on current knowledge and post-market experience.

4. **Identification of Potential Causes**: Determine the potential causes of identified hazardous situations, including software failures, user errors, environmental factors, and system interactions.

5. **Definition of New Risk Control Measures**: Define and implement new risk control measures as necessary to address identified hazards and maintain acceptable risk levels.

Documentation of these risk management activities shall be maintained in the risk management file per D0003103, Global Procedure, Risk Management.

#### 6.1.3.2 Gap Analysis Methodology (BS EN 62304 section 4.4.3)

A gap analysis shall be performed based on the Software Safety Classification to determine the required deliverables missing. The gap analysis shall:

1. **Inventory Existing Documentation**: Catalog all available development documentation, including requirements, design documents, test records, risk analyses, and configuration management records.

2. **Compare Against IEC 62304 Requirements**: Compare existing documentation against IEC 62304 requirements for the determined software safety class (A, B, or C) using Appendix 11.1 Legacy Software Gap Analysis Checklist.

3. **Document Identified Gaps**: Document specific gaps in requirements coverage, design documentation, verification activities, risk management, and configuration management.

4. **Prioritize Gaps by Risk**: Prioritize identified gaps based on their potential impact on software safety and regulatory compliance.

5. **Assess Gap Closure Feasibility**: Evaluate the feasibility and cost-effectiveness of closing each identified gap versus accepting residual risk.

#### 6.1.3.3 Gap Closure Plan and Execution (BS EN 62304 section 4.4.4)

A plan shall be established and executed to generate the identified deliverables. The gap closure plan shall include:

1. **Scope Definition**: Define which gaps will be closed and which will be accepted with appropriate risk justification.

2. **Timeline and Resources**: Establish realistic timelines and resource allocation for gap closure activities.

3. **Deliverable Specifications**: Specify the format, content, and acceptance criteria for each deliverable to be created.

4. **Risk Management Integration**: Ensure gap closure activities are integrated with ongoing risk management processes.

5. **Verification Strategy**: Define how newly created deliverables will be verified for completeness and accuracy.

6. **Execution Tracking**: Monitor progress against the plan and document any deviations or challenges encountered.

#### 6.1.3.4 Version Documentation and Rationale (BS EN 62304 section 4.4.5)

The version of the legacy software shall be documented with a rationale for the continued use. This documentation shall include:

1. **Version Identification**: Unique identification of the legacy software version, including build numbers, compilation dates, and configuration baselines.

2. **Rationale for Continued Use**: Comprehensive justification for continued use based on:
   - Risk-benefit analysis results
   - Gap analysis findings and closures
   - Post-market surveillance data
   - Clinical evidence of safety and effectiveness
   - Regulatory precedent and guidance

3. **Risk Assessment Summary**: Summary of residual risks associated with continued use and their acceptability.

4. **Maintenance Limitations**: Documentation of any limitations on maintenance activities for the legacy software.

5. **Obsolescence Evaluation**: Assessment of technology obsolescence risks and mitigation strategies.

## 6.2 Software Development Planning (BS EN 62304 section 5.1)

### Software Development Plan

A Software Development Plan shall be established for conducting activities appropriate to the scope, magnitude, and Software Safety Classification of the software system to be developed. The Software Development Plan may follow D0004168, Global Template, Software Development Plan, or equivalent. The plan shall be updated whenever appropriate throughout the development process.

Software Development Planning shall address the following:

- The Software Safety Classification (BS EN 62304)
- The processes and activities used to develop the Software System (e.g., SOP references, description of activities).
- Software deliverables (e.g., software requirements documents, software design, and architecture documents).
- A description of how the requirements will trace to higher-level system requirements, risk management, and testing (see Appendix 11.2 Requirements Traceability Requirements).
- The Software Problem Resolution Process used to describe how software anomalies and other issues discovered during software development are tracked and resolved.
- The plan for verifying software requirements, including verification tasks (e.g., protocol development, execution, reporting, regression), the associated milestones, and high-level acceptance criteria.
- Software risk management planning (this may reference the overall risk management plan if appropriate).
- A description or reference to the Software Configuration Management Plan.
- Prior to release of the software to production, the maintenance plan for software.

For Class B and C devices:

- A plan or reference to a plan for unit testing,
- Software integration and integration testing. It is acceptable to combine unit testing, integration testing and software testing into a single plan and set of activities.
- Identification and avoidance of common software defects which are introduced based on the selected programming technology that are relevant to the software system and providing evidence that these do not contribute to unacceptable risks.

For Class C devices:

- Standards, Methods, and Tools shall be included or referenced. (e.g., BS EN 62304, FDA Guidance, Internal Coding Standards)

## 6.3 Software Requirements (BS EN 62304 section 5.2)

Software Requirements shall be defined based on higher-level requirements and may be documented using D0004169, Global Template, Software Requirements, or equivalent. In situations where requirements management tools are used, the tool implementation shall ensure that the fields contained within D0004169, Global Template, Software Requirements, are part of the tool database.

Software Requirements shall be reviewed, and updated, when necessary, to ensure they do not contradict one another, are expressed in terms that avoid ambiguity, are stated in terms that permit establishment of test criteria and performance of tests, can be uniquely identified, and are traceable to higher-level requirements or other source documents.

### 6.3.1 Data Definition Requirements

For Class B and C devices, software requirements shall include definition of:

1. **Data Structures**: Definition of all data structures used by the software, including data types, ranges, and constraints.
2. **Data Flow**: Description of data flow between software components and external interfaces.
3. **Data Validation**: Requirements for input data validation, including range checks, format verification, and error handling.
4. **Data Persistence**: Requirements for data storage, backup, and recovery mechanisms.

### 6.3.2 Requirements Traceability

All software requirements shall be traceable to higher-level requirements (system requirements, user needs) and to verification activities (tests, reviews, analyses). Traceability shall be maintained using the methodology defined in Appendix 11.2 Requirements Traceability Requirements, which includes:

- Bidirectional traceability links
- Traceability matrix format and content requirements
- Approved traceability tools and methods
- Verification of traceability completeness
- Maintenance of traceability throughout development

For Class B and C devices:
Software Requirements shall include risk control measures that are to be implemented in software.

## 6.4 Software Architecture (BS EN 62304 section 5.3)

For Class B and C devices:
Software Architecture shall be developed based on the associated Software Requirements and risk controls to define major structural components of the software, their externally visible properties, and the relationship among them.
Software Architecture may be documented using D0004197, Global Template, Software Architecture, or equivalent.
Software Architecture shall include a description of SOUP items, including their functional and performance requirements, and a description of the hardware and software necessary to support the SOUP.
Software Architecture shall include a description of interfaces (e.g., communications, function calls) between different Software Items and between Software Items and hardware.

### 6.4.1 Risk Control Integration

The software architecture shall demonstrate integration of risk control measures by:

1. **Risk Control Allocation**: Clearly identifying which architectural components implement specific risk control measures.
2. **Segregation Design**: For Class C software, defining segregation between software items implementing risk controls and other software items.
3. **Interface Protection**: Defining how interfaces between components maintain risk control integrity.
4. **Failure Mode Design**: Describing how the architecture responds to component failures while maintaining safety.

Software Architecture shall be verified to confirm:

- The architecture of the software implements system and software requirements including those relating to risk control,
- The software architecture can support interfaces between software items and between software items and hardware, and
- The medical device architecture supports proper operation of any SOUP items.

For Class C devices:
Segregation between software items shall be identified for risk control and state how to ensure the segregation is effective.

## 6.5 Software Detailed Design (BS EN 62304 section 5.4)

For Class B and C devices:
Software Detailed Design shall be performed for all software units and may follow D0017986 Global Template, Software Detailed Design Document, or equivalent.
The software shall be subdivided until it is represented by software units.

For Class C devices:
The software detailed design shall include a description of interfaces between the Software Unit and external components (hardware and software) and a review of the software detailed design shall be conducted to verify that the software detailed design:

- Implements the software architecture, and
- Is free from contradiction with the software architecture.

## 6.6 Software Unit Implementation (BS EN 62304 section 5.5)

Code shall be developed based on Software Requirements, and when applicable, Software Architecture, and Software Detailed Design.

For Class B and C Software, the method for code review and acceptance criteria of unit implementation will be defined in the software development plan.

Unit-level testing shall be completed with the level of detail based on the risk of the software unit as defined in the software development plan.

### 6.6.1 General Unit Implementation Requirements

For all software safety classes, unit implementation shall include:

1. **Code Review**: Systematic review of source code for adherence to requirements, design, and coding standards.
2. **Unit Testing**: Testing of individual software units to verify correct implementation of unit-level requirements.
3. **Documentation**: Documentation of unit implementation including code comments, unit descriptions, and test results.

### 6.6.2 Class C Unit Implementation Requirements

For Class C software units, the following additional acceptance criteria shall be considered and addressed as appropriate to the software unit's risk:

1. **Target Environment Testing**: Verification that the software unit operates correctly in the target hardware and software environment, including:
   - Hardware timing constraints
   - Memory limitations
   - Operating system interactions
   - Compiler and toolchain dependencies

2. **Boundary Conditions**: Testing and analysis of software unit behavior at input and output boundaries, including:
   - Minimum and maximum input values
   - Data structure limits
   - Resource exhaustion scenarios
   - Invalid input handling

3. **Timing and Performance Verification**: Verification of software unit timing behavior, including:
   - Execution time measurement and analysis
   - Real-time constraint verification
   - Performance under load conditions
   - Timing-critical sequence verification

4. **Error Handling Verification**: Verification of error detection, isolation, and recovery mechanisms, including:
   - Error condition simulation
   - Error propagation analysis
   - Recovery mechanism testing
   - Graceful degradation verification

5. **Code Coverage Analysis**: Analysis of test coverage to ensure adequate testing of:
   - Statement coverage
   - Branch coverage
   - Path coverage (where applicable)
   - Modified Condition/Decision Coverage (MC/DC) for safety-critical units

For higher-risk Class C Software Units, the following additional acceptance criteria should be considered:

- proper event sequence,
- data and flow control,
- planned resource allocation,
- fault handling (error definition, isolation, and recovery),
- initialization of variables,
- self-diagnostics,
- memory management and memory overflows,
- boundary conditions.

## 6.7 Software Integration and Integration Testing (BS EN 62304 section 5.6)

For Class B and C items:
All Software Units shall be integrated per the integration plan defined in the Software Development Plan. Software Integration and Integration Testing shall be performed for all software Items. As applicable, integration tests shall demonstrate program behavior at the boundaries of its input and output domains and confirm program responses to invalid, unexpected, and special inputs.
As appropriate, regression testing shall be conducted during integration testing to demonstrate that defects have not been introduced into previously tested software.
Software Integration and Software System Testing may be combined into a single plan and set of activities.

## 6.8 Software System Testing (BS EN 62304 section 5.7)

Software System Testing shall be defined in the Software Development Plan. All software used for system verification shall be formally documented as released for testing (or equivalent, e.g., "Pilot") prior to the execution of testing. All software formally released for distribution shall have completed software and system verification prior to full release.

Software Verification Protocols shall be created that demonstrate program behavior at the boundaries of its input and output domains and confirm program responses to invalid, unexpected, and special inputs. Software Verification Protocols may use D0017982, Global Template, Software Verification Protocol, or equivalent, and D0017983, Global Template, Software Verification Report, or equivalent.

As appropriate, regression testing may be conducted during system testing to demonstrate that defects have not been introduced into previously tested software.

Anomalies detected during the software test cycle shall be maintained in accordance with the project's Software Problem Resolution Process defined in the Software Development Plan. Software anomalies detected during verification may be documented using D0004198 Global Template, Software Anomaly Report, or equivalent. Software anomalies may also be tracked from creation to closure using a defect tracking software (e.g., Jira, Azure DevOps).

### 6.8.1 System Test Traceability Requirements

Traceability of software requirements to software items and software testing results (including unit and integration testing as required) shall be documented and may use D0017985, Global Template, Software Traceability Matrix, or equivalent. The traceability documentation shall demonstrate:

1. **Requirements Coverage**: Every software requirement is verified by one or more tests.
2. **Test Coverage**: Every system test is traceable to one or more software requirements.
3. **Risk Control Verification**: Risk control measures implemented in software are verified by appropriate tests.
4. **Bidirectional Links**: Both forward traceability (requirements to tests) and backward traceability (tests to requirements) are maintained.

A formal design review shall be conducted to confirm the completion of the software deliverables defined in the Software Development Plan. This design review may be combined with other design reviews as appropriate. A report following D0017984, Global Template, Verification Summary Report, or equivalent, may be created to summarize the results of several Software Verification Reports, if appropriate.

Software Integration and System Testing may be combined into a single plan and set of activities.

## 6.9 Software Release (BS EN 62304 section 5.8)

The Software Development Plan shall document, or reference, the software release process, archival, and delivery to the point of use without corruption or unauthorized changes.
Software verification activities shall be completed prior to release to production.
Any known residual anomalies shall be documented.
All versions of software being released for verification testing or for production shall be documented. D0004199, Global Template, Version Description Document, or equivalent may be used to document known residual anomalies and the released version for testing.

For Class B and C devices:
All known unresolved anomalies shall also be evaluated to ensure they do not contribute to unacceptable risk and documented as part of D0004199 Global Template, Version Description Document, or equivalent.
The procedure and environment used to create the released software shall be documented.

## 6.10 Software Maintenance (BS EN 62304 section 6.1)

A Software Maintenance Plan shall be developed and may use D0004171, Global Template, Software Maintenance Plan, or equivalent. The plan shall address the following:

### 6.10.1 Feedback Monitoring and Analysis

1. **Feedback Reception**: Procedures for receiving, documenting, evaluating, resolving, and tracking feedback arising after software release, including:
   - Customer complaint systems integration
   - Field service feedback mechanisms
   - Post-market surveillance data collection
   - Healthcare provider reporting systems
   - User experience monitoring systems

2. **Feedback Analysis**: Systematic analysis of feedback to identify:
   - Software-related issues and trends
   - Usability problems
   - Performance degradation
   - Security vulnerabilities
   - Unintended use patterns

3. **Trend Analysis**: Regular analysis of feedback patterns to identify emerging issues before they become widespread problems.

### 6.10.2 Problem Classification and Response

Criteria for determining whether feedback is considered a problem (see Requirements for Complaint Management), including:

- Safety impact assessment criteria
- Performance impact thresholds
- Usability impact evaluation
- Regulatory reporting requirements
- Risk management integration

### 6.10.3 Maintenance Process Integration

1. **Risk Management Integration**: Use of the software risk management process for maintenance activities.
2. **Problem Resolution Integration**: Use of the Software Problem Resolution Process for analyzing and resolving problems arising after release of the software.
3. **Configuration Management Integration**: Use of the software configuration management process for managing modifications to the existing system.

### 6.10.4 SOUP Management

Procedures to evaluate and implement the upgrades, bug fixes, patches, and obsolescence of SOUP, including:

1. **SOUP Monitoring**: Systematic monitoring of SOUP vendors for updates, security patches, and end-of-life announcements.
2. **Update Evaluation**: Risk-based evaluation of SOUP updates for potential impact on device safety and performance.
3. **Regression Testing**: Requirements for regression testing when SOUP components are updated.
4. **Obsolescence Planning**: Planning for SOUP obsolescence and replacement strategies.

### 6.10.5 Regression Testing Documentation

For any software modifications, regression testing shall be performed and documented to demonstrate that:

1. **No New Defects**: The modification has not introduced new software defects.
2. **Preserved Functionality**: Previously verified functionality remains intact.
3. **Risk Control Integrity**: Risk control measures continue to function as intended.
4. **Performance Maintenance**: System performance has not been adversely affected.

Regression testing documentation shall include:

- Test plan identifying scope of regression testing
- Test procedures and acceptance criteria
- Test results and evidence
- Traceability to original verification activities
- Approval of regression test results

### 6.10.6 Regulatory Communication

As required by local regulation, the manufacturer may need to inform users and regulators regarding any problem with the released software and consequence of continued use along with the nature of any available software, how to obtain, and install these changes.

## 6.11 Software-Specific Elements of the Risk Management Process (BS EN 62304 section 7)

Risk analysis of products shall be in accordance with the overall project Risk Management Plan per D0003103 Global Procedure, Risk Management.

### 6.11.1 Risk Management Integration Workflow

Risk management activities shall be integrated throughout the software development lifecycle at the following integration points:

#### Planning Phase Integration (BS EN 62304 section 5.1)

1. **Software Development Plan**: Include software risk management approach, including hazard identification methodology and risk control strategy.
2. **Risk Management File Establishment**: Initialize software-specific sections of the risk management file.
3. **Hazard Analysis Planning**: Plan for software hazard analysis activities and resource allocation.

#### Requirements Phase Integration (BS EN 62304 section 5.2)

1. **Requirements Risk Analysis**: Analyze software requirements for potential contribution to hazardous situations.
2. **Risk Control Requirements**: Include risk control measures as software requirements.
3. **Traceability Establishment**: Establish traceability from hazards to risk control requirements.

#### Architecture Phase Integration (BS EN 62304 section 5.3)

1. **Architecture Risk Review**: Review software architecture for risk control implementation.
2. **Segregation Design**: For Class C software, design segregation to isolate risk control functions.
3. **Risk Control Allocation**: Allocate risk control measures to specific architectural components.

#### Design Phase Integration (BS EN 62304 section 5.4)

1. **Detailed Design Risk Analysis**: Analyze detailed design for risk control implementation completeness.
2. **Interface Risk Analysis**: Analyze software unit interfaces for potential hazards.
3. **Design Review Risk Assessment**: Include risk management perspective in design reviews.

#### Implementation Phase Integration (BS EN 62304 section 5.5)

1. **Code Review Risk Focus**: Include risk control implementation verification in code reviews.
2. **Unit Test Risk Coverage**: Ensure unit testing covers risk control functionality.
3. **Risk Control Verification**: Verify risk control measures are correctly implemented.

#### Integration Phase Integration (BS EN 62304 section 5.6)

1. **Integration Risk Testing**: Test risk control measures during software integration.
2. **Interface Risk Verification**: Verify interfaces do not compromise risk controls.
3. **System-Level Risk Assessment**: Assess integrated software system for emergent risks.

#### System Testing Phase Integration (BS EN 62304 section 5.7)

1. **Risk Control Verification**: Verify all risk control measures function as intended.
2. **Hazard Scenario Testing**: Test software response to identified hazardous scenarios.
3. **Risk Control Traceability**: Verify traceability from hazards through controls to verification.

#### Release Phase Integration (BS EN 62304 section 5.8)

1. **Residual Risk Evaluation**: Evaluate and document residual risks before release.
2. **Risk Management File Completion**: Complete software sections of risk management file.
3. **Risk Communication**: Prepare risk communication materials for users and regulators.

#### Maintenance Phase Integration (BS EN 62304 section 6.1)

1. **Post-Market Risk Monitoring**: Monitor post-market data for new or changing risks.
2. **Change Risk Assessment**: Assess risks of software changes and modifications.
3. **Risk Management File Updates**: Update risk management file based on post-market experience.

### 6.11.2 Software Hazard Analysis Process

Software hazard analysis shall identify software items that may contribute to a hazardous situation involving software, including SOUP. The hazard analysis process shall include:

1. **Hazard Identification**: Systematic identification of ways the software could contribute to hazardous situations, including:
   - Software failure modes
   - Incorrect software behavior
   - Software-hardware interaction failures
   - User interface hazards
   - SOUP-related hazards

2. **Hazard Analysis**: For each identified hazard, analyze:
   - Potential causes and contributing factors
   - Sequence of events leading to hazardous situation
   - Severity of potential harm
   - Probability of occurrence
   - Detection and mitigation capabilities

3. **Risk Evaluation**: Evaluate risks using the methodology defined in D0003103 Global Procedure, Risk Management.

### 6.11.3 Risk Control Measures

If a risk control measure is implemented in the software, it shall be included in the Software Requirements.

A software safety class shall be assigned to software Items based on the possible effects of the risk that the risk control measure is controlling.

If a risk control measure is implemented as a software Item, any new sequence of events that could result in a hazardous situation shall be identified in the Risk Management File – See D0003103 Global Procedure, Risk Management.

### 6.11.4 Risk Control Verification Process

For Class B and C software, risk control measures shall be verified through:

1. **Verification Planning**: Define verification methods for each risk control measure, including:
   - Testing strategies
   - Analysis methods
   - Review criteria
   - Acceptance criteria

2. **Verification Execution**: Execute verification activities per the plan, documenting:
   - Test procedures and results
   - Analysis reports
   - Review outcomes
   - Non-conformance resolution

3. **Verification Documentation**: Document verification evidence demonstrating:
   - Risk control measures are correctly implemented
   - Risk control measures are effective under normal and abnormal conditions
   - Risk control measures do not introduce unacceptable risks

### 6.11.5 Risk Traceability

For Class B and C Software:
Traceability of software hazards shall be documented as appropriate from the hazardous situation to the software item to the specific software cause to the risk control measure to the verification of the risk control measure.

The traceability documentation shall demonstrate:

1. **Hazard-to-Software Item**: Links from identified hazards to specific software items that could contribute.
2. **Software Item-to-Cause**: Links from software items to specific failure modes or causes.
3. **Cause-to-Control**: Links from causes to implemented risk control measures.
4. **Control-to-Verification**: Links from risk control measures to verification activities and evidence.

### 6.11.6 Residual Risk Evaluation

After implementation and verification of risk control measures, residual risks shall be evaluated including:

1. **Residual Risk Assessment**: Evaluate remaining risks after risk control implementation.
2. **Risk-Benefit Analysis**: For residual risks that cannot be reduced further, perform risk-benefit analysis per ISO 14971 §4.5.
3. **Risk Acceptability**: Document acceptability of residual risks based on risk-benefit analysis.
4. **Risk Communication**: Plan communication of residual risks to users through labeling, instructions, and training.

### 6.11.7 Cybersecurity Risk Management

The process for managing risks associated with data and security shall be defined in or referenced from the Software Development Plan. The process shall provide a methodology for identifying vulnerabilities, estimating, and evaluating the associated threats, controlling these threats, and monitoring the effectiveness of the risk controls. See D0029257, Global Work Instruction, Cybersecurity for Medical Devices for details.

## 6.12 Software Configuration Management (BS EN 62304 section 8)

A Software Configuration Management Plan shall be established for the software development, and may follow D0004170, Global Template, Software Configuration Management Plan, or equivalent.

The Software Configuration Management Plan shall address:

- Tools used in developing the software (e.g., compilers, bug trackers).
- A description of how the software is controlled, including how changes are managed, and how SOUP is handled.
- Categories (or lists) of items that will be controlled.
- Tasks and activities related to Configuration Management.

For Class B and C devices:
How items that support software development are controlled. Examples may include software tools that require validation and control (in accordance with D0003078, Global Procedure, Non-Device Software Validation). (e.g., compiler/assembler versions, make files, batch files, environment settings)

### 6.12.1 SOUP Configuration Management

For software systems containing SOUP items, the configuration management plan shall include specific provisions for SOUP management:

1. **SOUP Identification**: Unique identification of all SOUP items including:
   - SOUP name and version
   - Vendor/supplier information
   - License information
   - Security patch level
   - Installation configuration

2. **SOUP List Maintenance**: Maintenance of a comprehensive SOUP inventory using D0017987 Global Template, SOUP List, or equivalent, including:
   - Current SOUP versions in use
   - Approved SOUP versions
   - End-of-life status
   - Security vulnerability status
   - Update/patch history

3. **SOUP Change Control**: Procedures for managing SOUP updates, including:
   - Impact assessment process
   - Approval requirements
   - Testing requirements
   - Documentation updates
   - Rollback procedures

4. **SOUP Obsolescence Management**: Procedures for managing SOUP obsolescence, including:
   - End-of-life monitoring
   - Migration planning
   - Risk assessment of continued use
   - Alternative evaluation

Software configuration management provides the ability to recreate a software item, to identify its constituent parts, and to provide a history of the changes that have been made to it.

Configuration Identification shall be governed by the Software Configuration Management Plan, including management of all configuration items (e.g., source code, documentation, SOUP). There shall be a means to identify configuration items by unique name and version number. This scheme shall include other Software Products or entities such as SOUP and documentation.

The configuration items (e.g., source code, documentation, SOUP) shall be maintained in validated, controlled, and auditable repositories (e.g., Agile) for at least the life of the Software Product, or as long as the product is supported for human use, whichever comes first.

## 6.13 Software Problem Resolution Process (BS EN 62304 section 9)

The Software Development Plan shall address the handling of software anomalies.

Once the product is developed and in production, anomalies shall be handled in accordance with the Software Maintenance Plan. Any complaints for which an action is needed by the software team shall be logged and tracked until it is dispositioned. See D0003325, Global Procedure, Nonconformance as required consideration of software related complaint escalation.

All anomalies identified shall be investigated, evaluated for safety, and will result in a change request or rationale for taking no action. Any actions taken to resolve the anomaly shall be verified and ensure the change did not result in additional anomalies.

### 6.13.1 Problem Trend Analysis Process

A systematic process for problem trend analysis shall be established to identify patterns in software problems that may indicate systemic issues. The trend analysis process shall include:

1. **Data Collection**: Collection of problem data from multiple sources including:
   - Software anomaly reports
   - Customer complaints
   - Field service reports
   - Post-market surveillance data
   - Internal testing findings

2. **Trend Identification**: Regular analysis (at least quarterly) to identify trends such as:
   - Increasing frequency of similar problems
   - Problems clustered around specific software versions
   - Problems associated with specific use cases or environments
   - Recurring root causes

3. **Trend Evaluation**: Evaluation of identified trends for:
   - Safety implications
   - Regulatory impact
   - Need for corrective action
   - Prevention opportunities

4. **Corrective and Preventive Actions**: Implementation of corrective and preventive actions (CAPA) based on trend analysis findings, including:
   - Process improvements
   - Design changes
   - Training enhancements
   - Documentation updates

5. **Trend Analysis Documentation**: Documentation of trend analysis activities, findings, and actions taken, maintained as part of the quality management system.

### 6.13.2 Problem Verification Requirements

For all problem resolutions involving software changes, verification shall be performed to ensure:

1. **Problem Resolution Verification**: The implemented change actually resolves the identified problem.
2. **No New Problems**: The change has not introduced new software anomalies or problems.
3. **Requirements Compliance**: The change maintains compliance with all applicable software requirements.
4. **Risk Control Integrity**: The change does not compromise existing risk control measures.
5. **Performance Impact**: The change does not adversely affect software performance or functionality.

Verification methods may include testing, analysis, inspection, or demonstration, as appropriate to the nature and risk of the change.

## Appendix 10.1 Software Safety Classification

A software product may contain multiple software systems, and each system may contain multiple software items. The classification process, based on risk, determines the safety class for each software system and each software item. From this comprehensive classification, the software deliverables may be appropriately tailored for a given project. Claims for any A or B classification analysis/actions shall be documented to support the claims.

### Medical Device Classification

The following table defines the software safety classifications for Acme medical devices.

**Software Classification A:**

The SOFTWARE SYSTEM cannot contribute to a HAZARDOUS SITUATION; or
The SOFTWARE SYSTEM can contribute to a HAZARDOUS SITUATION which does not result in unacceptable RISK after consideration of RISK CONTROL measures external to the SOFTWARE SYSTEM.

**Software Classification B:**

The SOFTWARE SYSTEM can contribute to a HAZARDOUS SITUATION which results in unacceptable RISK after consideration of RISK CONTROL measures external to the SOFTWARE SYSTEM and the resulting possible HARM is non-SERIOUS INJURY.

**Software Classification C:**

The SOFTWARE SYSTEM can contribute to a HAZARDOUS SITUATION which results in unacceptable RISK after consideration of RISK CONTROL measures external to the SOFTWARE SYSTEM and the resulting possible HARM is death or SERIOUS INJURY.

## Appendix 11.2 Requirements Traceability Requirements

This appendix defines the requirements traceability methodology to ensure compliance with IEC 62304 sections 5.2.6 and 5.7.1.

### Traceability Matrix Format

The requirements traceability matrix shall include the following information for each software requirement:

1. **Requirement Information**:
   - Unique requirement identifier
   - Requirement text or summary
   - Requirement source (system requirement, user need, regulation)
   - Requirement priority/criticality
   - Software safety class applicability

2. **Design Traceability**:
   - Software architecture elements implementing the requirement
   - Detailed design elements (Class B/C)
   - Software units implementing the requirement

3. **Verification Traceability**:
   - Unit tests verifying the requirement (Class B/C)
   - Integration tests verifying the requirement
   - System tests verifying the requirement
   - Other verification methods (analysis, inspection, demonstration)

4. **Risk Management Traceability**:
   - Related hazards or hazardous situations
   - Risk control measures (if applicable)
   - Risk control verification activities

### Approved Traceability Tools

The following tools are approved for maintaining requirements traceability:

1. **D0017985 Global Template, Software Traceability Matrix** - For smaller projects or when tool-based solutions are not available
2. **Requirements management tools** integrated with the development environment
3. **Application Lifecycle Management (ALM) tools** that provide integrated traceability

### Traceability Verification Process

Traceability shall be verified through:

1. **Forward Traceability Check**: Verify that every software requirement is traced to:
   - At least one design element
   - At least one verification activity
   - Risk control measures (where applicable)

2. **Backward Traceability Check**: Verify that every:
   - Design element traces to at least one requirement
   - Verification activity traces to at least one requirement
   - Risk control measure traces to at least one hazard and requirement

3. **Orphan Analysis**: Identify and resolve:
   - Requirements without design implementation
   - Requirements without verification
   - Design elements not traced to requirements
   - Verification activities not traced to requirements

### Traceability Maintenance

Traceability shall be maintained throughout the development lifecycle:

1. **During Requirements Changes**: Update all affected traces when requirements are added, modified, or deleted
2. **During Design Changes**: Update design traces when architecture or detailed design changes
3. **During Implementation**: Update implementation traces when software units are modified
4. **During Testing**: Update verification traces when tests are added, modified, or results change

## Appendix 11.3 SOUP Management Requirements

This appendix defines the requirements for managing Software of Unknown Provenance (SOUP) items in medical device software.

### SOUP Identification Requirements

All SOUP items shall be identified and documented including:

1. **Basic Information**:
   - SOUP name and version number
   - Vendor/supplier name and contact information
   - License type and restrictions
   - Operating system and platform requirements

2. **Functional Information**:
   - Functional requirements satisfied by SOUP
   - Performance requirements and limitations
   - Interface specifications
   - Dependencies on other software or hardware

3. **Risk Information**:
   - Known anomalies and limitations
   - Security vulnerabilities and patch status
   - End-of-life status and support timeline
   - Risk assessment results

### SOUP List Template (D0017987)

A comprehensive SOUP list shall be maintained using the following format:

| SOUP Item | Version   | Vendor   | License   | Function  | Risk Class | Status        | Last Updated |
| --------- | --------- | -------- | --------- | --------- | ---------- | ------------- | ------------ |
| [Name]    | [Version] | [Vendor] | [License] | [Purpose] | [A/B/C]    | [Current/EOL] | [Date]       |

### SOUP Configuration Management

SOUP items shall be managed under configuration control including:

1. **Version Control**: Specific versions of SOUP approved for use
2. **Change Control**: Process for evaluating and approving SOUP updates
3. **Installation Control**: Standardized installation and configuration procedures
4. **Documentation Control**: Maintenance of SOUP-related documentation

### SOUP Risk Assessment

Each SOUP item shall undergo risk assessment including:

1. **Hazard Analysis**: Identification of how SOUP failures could contribute to hazardous situations
2. **Risk Evaluation**: Assessment of risks associated with SOUP use
3. **Risk Control**: Implementation of measures to control SOUP-related risks
4. **Risk Monitoring**: Ongoing monitoring of SOUP-related risks and vulnerabilities
